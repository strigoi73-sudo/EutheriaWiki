# States

The states of Eutheria are shaped by geography, trade access, religion, and the long aftereffects of older imperial and civilizational orders. Some are large agrarian powers, others strategic coastal or sacred polities whose importance far exceeds their land area.

This section is one of the most practical ways to enter the setting because state pages bring together geography, history, religion, and diplomacy in a single place. Readers who want stronger context may want to pair these articles with [Geography](../geography/index.md) and [History](../history/index.md), especially the [Veltric Empire and the Age of Fracture](../history/veltric-empire-and-age-of-fracture.md), which explains why so many modern polities define themselves in relation to older imperial collapse.

The range of political forms across Eutheria is wide. [Likia](likia.md) is a major maritime power shaped by naval conflict and continental rivalry, while [Polbium Theocracy](polbium-theocracy.md) shows how state legitimacy can be inseparable from religious authority. Eastern states such as [Han Guo](han-guo.md), [Kaihui](kaihui.md), and [Guan Guo](guan-guo.md), alongside southern powers such as [Fon](fon.md), illustrate political traditions that do not map neatly onto western post-Veltric assumptions.

State articles are meant to function as strong reference pages rather than mere directories. They identify where a polity sits, what defines its political or social character, and how it relates to surrounding powers. Where the source maps support it, major state articles also include cropped map excerpts so readers can immediately place them in regional context.
