# Cosmology

Cosmology in Eutheria describes more than the structure of the universe. It explains the metaphysical conditions of existence itself: the origin of souls, the role of free will, the nature of the planes, and the relationship between ordered reality and the annihilating threat beyond it.

This section records the core metaphysical framework of the setting in reference form. These articles matter not only for theology, but for death, magic, outsiders, alignment, and the limits of mortal understanding.

## Major themes

- ordered existence begins in the **First Moment**
- the **Pleroma** is the source of souls and primordial unity
- the **Material Plane** exists in equilibrium among larger alignment forces
- the cycle of mortal life, death, and postmortem transformation is central to understanding souls
- the **Kenoma** and the **Outer Gods** represent something beyond destruction within the ordinary cosmic order
- divine and arcane spellcasting are structured consequences of cosmic architecture
