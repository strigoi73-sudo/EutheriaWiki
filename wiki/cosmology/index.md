# Cosmology

Eutherian cosmology describes the origin and structure of reality, the arrangement of the planes, the cycle of the soul, the nature of divine and magical action, and the existential danger associated with the outer void. It is the metaphysical foundation beneath both religion and magic, and many ideas that appear in political or religious writing only make full sense when placed back into this wider frame.

## Structure of Reality

The best place to begin is [First Moment and the Structure of Reality](first-moment-and-the-structure-of-reality.md), which explains the world's basic metaphysical architecture. From there, [Planes of Eutheria](planes-of-eutheria.md) describes the arrangement of material, inner, outer, and transitive realms, while [Souls and the Cycle of Existence](souls-and-the-cycle-of-existence.md) clarifies what souls are, how they move after death, and why mortal existence sits inside a larger cosmic order.

This structure is not merely philosophical background. It determines how afterlives are imagined, how divine power is understood, and why many faiths treat death, transfiguration, and alignment as objective features of reality rather than as symbolic language alone.

## Magic and Divine Order

[Divine Power and Magic](divine-power-and-magic.md) is the strongest bridge between cosmology and lived experience. Magic in Eutheria is not an inexplicable exception to the world, but a normal consequence of the world's structure and its saturation with astral substance. Divine action, innate magical traits, bloodlines, and magical craft all belong to the same underlying metaphysical order, even when different cultures interpret them through different theological vocabularies.

## The Outer Threat

The cosmological frame also includes the danger represented by [Kenoma and the Outer Gods](kenoma-and-the-outer-gods.md). Public-facing cosmology acknowledges a hostile outer dimension and the possibility of metaphysical unmaking, but it does not collapse all mystery into direct explanation. The glossary articles on [Kenoma](../glossary/kenoma.md) and [Pleroma](../glossary/pleroma.md) are useful anchors here, because those terms recur across the whole setting whenever writers discuss fullness, order, void, or transcendence.

Taken together, these articles explain why cosmology matters so much in Eutheria. It is not a decorative theological appendix. It is the deepest explanatory layer available to public knowledge.
