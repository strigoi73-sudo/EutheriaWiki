# Thousand-Year War

The Thousand-Year War was the great ancient conflict between the surface civilizations of the Free Folk and the Deep Folk alliance of Drow, Hryngar, and major orc war bands. It lasted roughly a millennium and ended at the [Battle of Trelhelm](great-catastrophe-and-trelhelm.md) in **9034 BLC**.

## Deep Folk crisis

Public historiography holds that mounting pressures in the deep underworld destabilized Drow and Hryngar societies and drove them toward large-scale war against the surface world. The exact cause of those pressures remains disputed.

Surviving accounts describe scarcity, deep disturbances, and worsening instability beneath the earth, but they do not agree on a single explanation. Some traditions treat the crisis as ecological, others as magical, and still others as something no later age has fully reconstructed.

## Scope of the conflict

The war drew in nearly every major civilization of the age. Ancient cities were destroyed, populations were displaced, and repeated campaigns brought the Free Folk close to collapse more than once.

The Deep Folk alliance was never culturally uniform, but wartime necessity aligned subterranean powers with powerful orc confederations in a common military effort against the surface.

## Trelhelm and aftermath

In the war's final phase, the orc warlord TarKath unified major Dark Folk forces. His defeat at Trelhelm broke coalition momentum and ended the war.

After the victory:

- Drow withdrew fully into the depths
- Hryngar retreated back into deep subterranean strongholds
- Orc populations remained partly on or near the surface in frontier regions

The end of the war coincided with the beginning of the Great Withdrawal, the long glacial retreat that transformed the physical world during the same broad era of recovery.

## Historical importance

The Thousand-Year War matters not only because of its scale, but because so much later history unfolded in its shadow. It devastated early civilizations, interrupted continuity of knowledge, and shaped how later peoples remembered both ancient threat and hard-won survival.
