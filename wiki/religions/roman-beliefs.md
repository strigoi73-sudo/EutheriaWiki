# Roman Beliefs

Roman Beliefs are the organized polytheist tradition of the older Veltric republican era. They developed out of the older animist-folk layer now called [Old Roman Beliefs](old-roman-beliefs.md), but became more formalized and more closely associated with state organization.

## Historical role

Before the imperial religious order consolidated around the [Rocciaganel Church](rocciaganel-church.md), Roman Beliefs served as the principal state religion of the republican Roman-analog sphere.

## Post-imperial resurgence

Roman Beliefs have experienced renewed popularity since the collapse of the Veltric Empire. This revival is both religious and political: in many successor regions, renewed adherence also signals rejection of over-centralized ecclesiastical inheritance.

## Public significance

Among organized traditions in eastern Nereth, Roman Beliefs are notable for their current vitality and missionary confidence. Their expansionist energy helps explain why the religious politics of the Fracture are not merely about survival of old institutions, but about active competition over the future moral language of post-imperial society.
