# Berbers of Kasmora

The Berbers of Kasmora are a major civilizational people distributed across multiple political contexts, with [Haria](../states/haria.md) as their principal state center. They are defined by shared historical memory, cultural continuity, and regional rootedness rather than by a single border.

## Overview

Berber communities in Kasmora form an enduring people whose identity predates and exceeds current state arrangements. Their cohesion comes from long-standing social traditions, language continuities, and institutional memory.

## Distribution across states

While Haria is the most visible political expression of Berber power, Berber populations also exist beyond Harian sovereignty. This distribution makes Berber identity both politically central and trans-boundary.

## Historical distinctiveness

Berbers are historically distinct for sustaining civilizational continuity through shifting regional balances, including periods of imperial pressure, maritime competition, and religious divergence in neighboring polities.

## Geography, power, and communal continuity

Coastal and inland settlement patterns have produced varied local forms of Berber life. State power influences these communities, but does not erase shared belonging. The people remain a civilizational category in their own right.
