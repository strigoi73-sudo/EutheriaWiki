# Taizhouism

Taizhouism is a major religious-philosophical tradition associated with Chinese-classed societies in Valthera, with its sacred historical center at **Shang** in [Quz Guo](../states/quz-guo.md).

In current canon usage, Taizhouism matters both as a religious system and as a source of political legitimacy for neighboring states, especially [Han Guo](../states/han-guo.md).

## Sacred geography

Shang is treated as a foundational Taizhouist site. The continued sovereignty of Quz Guo is tied in part to preserving Shang's sacred standing outside ordinary secular provincial administration.

This gives Taizhouism a concrete geopolitical dimension: sacred-site governance and interstate protection obligations are part of how the tradition is sustained.

## Political significance

Han Guo's regional role includes the protection of Quz Guo and Shang, framed as religious duty rather than pure strategic convenience. Taizhouism therefore operates not only in temple and doctrine, but also in constitutional identity and regional diplomatic order.

## Current limitations

Detailed encyclopedia-level treatment remains incomplete in several areas, including:

- canonical texts and doctrinal schools
- clergy and institutional hierarchy
- ritual calendar and major observances
- variation between state-sponsored and local practice
