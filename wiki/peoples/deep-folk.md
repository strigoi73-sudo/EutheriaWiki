# Deep Folk

The Deep Folk are the broad underworld adversaries associated with the Deep Folk crisis and the Thousand-Year War. In public history, they include [drow](drow.md), [hryngar](hryngar.md), and [orc](orcs.md) groups whose alliance nearly broke the surface world in the ancient era.

The term describes a historical and strategic threat more than a single unified people in the present. It is a category created by war and memory rather than a precise ethnographic label. The peoples grouped under it did not share a single culture or political order, but they did become associated with a common underworld crisis and a nearly catastrophic invasion of the surface.

In ordinary historiography, the Deep Folk crisis began when pressures of unknown origin destabilized the deepest underworld regions, pushing drow and hryngar societies into unsustainable conditions. These peoples allied with powerful orc groups and launched the invasions that became the Thousand-Year War. The final defeat of that coalition at Trelhelm drove the surviving drow and hryngar back below, while many orc groups remained nearer the surface in frontier zones.
