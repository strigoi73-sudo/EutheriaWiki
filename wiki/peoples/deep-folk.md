# Deep Folk

Deep Folk is the broad historical term for the subterranean peoples most closely associated with the ancient underworld crisis that led to the [Thousand-Year War](../history/thousand-year-war.md). In standard usage it refers primarily to the Drow, the Hryngar, and the major orc forces that fought alongside them during that conflict.

## Historical meaning

The term is not a statement that these peoples formed one culture. It is a historical grouping used for the wartime alliance that opposed the surface civilizations of the Free Folk.

## Crisis and war

Public history holds that severe pressures arose in the deep underworld, destabilizing subterranean societies and helping drive the great conflict of the age. The exact cause of those pressures remains debated.

What is clear is that the resulting war was large enough to threaten surface civilization itself, and long enough to shape the memory of multiple peoples for millennia.

## After Trelhelm

After the defeat of TarKath at Trelhelm:

- Drow withdrew fully into the depths
- Hryngar retreated back into deep subterranean strongholds
- Orcs remained more visible on or near the surface frontier

This divergence helps explain why "Deep Folk" is mainly a historical and strategic category rather than a stable contemporary identity.
