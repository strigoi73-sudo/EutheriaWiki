# Quz Guo

Quz Guo is a small sovereign Chinese Taizhouist state centered on **Shang**, the birthplace of Taizhouist philosophy and religion.

It predates [Han Guo](han-guo.md) and is best understood as an older sacred city-state rather than a later political creation of the republic.

## Political status

Quz Guo is formally sovereign and governs itself internally, but its external security and geopolitical shelter depend heavily on Han Guo.

Its continued independence is explained primarily by sacred status. In canon framing, absorption into ordinary secular administration would diminish the religious standing of Shang.

## Relationship with Han Guo

Han Guo's protection of Quz Guo is treated as a religious duty, not just treaty convenience. The resulting arrangement is dual but coherent: Insan functions as republican political capital, while Shang remains a sacred center of the wider Taizhouist world.

## Current limitations

Several encyclopedia-level details remain open, including:

- internal governing institutions and clerical-civic balance
- territorial extent beyond the Shang-centered core
- formal legal basis of Han Guo security guarantees
