# The Tome and Void Knowledge

The Veltric manuscript is the Necronomicon, or a recurrence, recension, or derivative manifestation of the same body of void knowledge. Public scholarship only knows it as a forbidden late-imperial manuscript. The hidden truth is that it is accurate.

## Identity

The tome is the work attributed to Abdul Alhazred, the mad poet of Sanaa, known in another universe within the infinite Material Plane as a work of horror fiction.

In Eutheria's universe there is no fictional frame protecting readers from what it describes. Its account of the Kenoma, the entities native to it, and the means of contacting them is real.

## Provenance

How the tome crossed from one universe within the Material Plane to another is unknown and may be unknowable. It need not be the exact same physical object once suppressed after the ancient crisis. It may be a later recurrence of the same forbidden knowledge-stream.

## Why it breaks people

The tome does not merely teach dangerous facts. It imposes an intolerable perception of incompleteness behind existence itself. Mortal minds cannot hold that knowledge without fracturing.

That fracture is the gateway condition for modern cult formation.

## Historical role

The late-Veltric translation produced the modern Cult of the Void, but the tome's deeper significance is that it reconnects the present crisis to the ancient underworld precursor behind the Thousand-Year War.

## Current status

Its current location is known only to the Cult of the Void.
