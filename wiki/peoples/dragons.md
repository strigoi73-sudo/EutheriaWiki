# Dragons

Dragons are among the oldest intelligent beings in Eutherian history and the first great apex civilization known to the world. Their earliest age predates the rise of the humanoid peoples by vast spans of time, and their place in Eutheria is both biological and cosmological.

## Historical role

The age of dragons shaped the oldest civilizational strata of the world. Dragon history cannot be separated from the later Dragon-Giant Wars, whose collapse broke the first great ancient powers and opened the way for later surface civilizations. Ancient reptilian temple cultures, ceremonial ruins, and draconic sacred memory all belong to this long pre-humanoid world.

## Lineages and cosmological significance

Dragons are believed to descend from a single ancestral lineage of linnorm-like proto-dragons. Through prolonged interaction with astral forces, that lineage developed extraordinary intelligence, innate magic, and limited free will. Unlike most mortal creatures, dragons are innately bound to specific alignments, and their souls transfigure reliably into corresponding outsiders at death. For that reason, dragons are understood as the first mortals whose souls became outsiders at all.

Chromatic and metallic dragons are the major evil- and good-aligned branches known in the current age. Imperial dragons are remembered as a separate and much rarer branch associated with cosmic forces, while primal dragons are not native to the Material Plane and appear only exceptionally.

## Golsta

In the present day, [Golsta](../geography/golsta.md) contains the largest surviving concentrations of dragons, especially cold-adapted lineages such as white and silver dragons. Its severe conditions and limited humanoid settlement make it one of the last places where substantial draconic populations can persist without constant pressure from younger civilizations.
