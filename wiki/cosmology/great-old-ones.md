# Great Old Ones

Great Old Ones are cosmological threats associated with the [Kenoma](kenoma-and-outer-gods.md) and with forms of pressure that ordinary mortal theology and planar scholarship struggle to explain. They are not treated as ordinary monsters, even when game rules can represent encounters with them.

## Cosmological status

Current canon presents Great Old Ones as localized crystallizations of Outer God pressure within ordered existence. They are therefore more legible than the Outer Gods themselves, but not safer, simpler, or fully comprehensible.

## Public knowledge

Knowledge about Great Old Ones is fragmentary, contradictory, and often distorted. Divination, scholarship, and inherited religious language all have limits when dealing with them.

For that reason, public discourse usually treats them as apocalyptic occult entities rather than as subjects that can be comfortably integrated into normal theology, bestiary classification, or political planning.

## Threat character

Great Old Ones matter because they imply more than regional danger. They suggest points where the deeper instability beyond the ordered cosmos becomes locally present in ways that mortal beings can actually encounter.
