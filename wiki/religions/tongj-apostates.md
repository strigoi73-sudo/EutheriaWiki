# Tongj Apostates

The Tongj Apostates are a Valtheran religious tradition associated with prolonged frontier contact between Korean-analog and Dwarven populations, especially in upland regions connected to [Hayanguk](../states/hayanguk.md).

Current canon treats the tradition as evidence that Valthera's mountain frontiers are active zones of reinterpretation rather than fixed civilizational boundaries.

## Historical context

The Tongj Apostates are best read within a long frontier process, not as an isolated doctrinal event. Their emergence supports a broader Valtheran pattern in which exchange corridors, upland settlements, and mixed communities generate enduring religious innovation.

## Religious profile

At present, the tradition is canonically significant for what it demonstrates structurally: contact-driven theology, identity negotiation across language communities, and institutional adaptation under frontier pressure.

It is not primarily represented as a conversionist imperial creed. Instead, it marks how regional religious systems can develop in contested borderlands where no single polity controls all social authority.

## Civilizational significance

The Tongj Apostates reinforce a key interpretation of Valthera: religion, politics, and culture do not map into clean territorial blocks. Frontier traditions can become central to continental identity even when originating outside major heartland capitals.

## Current limitations

Many details remain open in encyclopedia-level canon, including:

- formal doctrine and liturgical structure
- clerical hierarchy and institutional centers
- geographic extent beyond Hayanguk-linked frontier zones
- relations with larger regional religious traditions
