# Religions

Religion in Eutheria is not a private sphere separated cleanly from politics, social order, or metaphysical belief. It shapes kingship, funerary custom, education, law, pilgrimage, diplomacy, and the moral language through which communities explain both authority and suffering. The world contains continent-spanning organized traditions, region-bound religious landscapes, and state-linked institutions whose influence often extends well beyond their formal borders.

## Religion and Worldview

Most Eutherian religions are inseparable from cosmological assumptions about the soul, divine order, and the structure of reality. Readers who want the deeper metaphysical frame should keep [Cosmology](../cosmology/index.md) close at hand, especially [Divine Power and Magic](../cosmology/divine-power-and-magic.md), [Souls and the Cycle of Existence](../cosmology/souls-and-the-cycle-of-existence.md), and the glossary entries for [Pleroma](../glossary/pleroma.md) and [Kenoma](../glossary/kenoma.md). Even when traditions disagree sharply, they often do so inside a world where divine agency and sacred order are taken seriously.

## Major Religious Traditions

[Skrosenism](skrosenism.md) is the largest organized religion in the world and one of the most institutionally flexible. Its decentralized temple structure, local variation in primary deity, and broad cultural spread help explain why it can function both as ancestral faith and as a transregional civilizational system. The [Rocciaganel Church](rocciaganel-church.md), by contrast, represents a more centralized ecclesiastical tradition whose importance is bound tightly to imperial legacy, hierarchy, and public authority in the post-Veltric world.

[Ayedism](ayedism.md), [Delistanism](delistanism.md), and [Taizhouism](taizhouism.md) show other major models of sacred life. They differ in theology, social embedding, and institutional form, but all demonstrate that religion in Eutheria helps organize entire ways of life rather than merely personal belief. The [Brinese Order](brinese-order.md) and [Eastern Nereth Religious Landscape](eastern-nereth-religious-landscape.md) are especially useful for understanding how religion becomes regional texture, shaping moral order, ritual expectations, and local identity.

## Religion and Power

Religious affiliation often overlaps with legitimacy, but not always in the same way. Some states are overtly organized through sacred authority, while others remain politically secular in form yet culturally saturated by a dominant faith. In some regions, confessional mismatch is itself a political fact, as when state structures, dominant populations, and religious institutions do not align neatly. That mismatch is one reason religion in Eutheria so often appears in border questions, educational systems, factional politics, and inherited grievances.

This section is therefore best approached as more than a catalog of beliefs. It is a guide to the moral and institutional orders that make Eutherian societies intelligible.
