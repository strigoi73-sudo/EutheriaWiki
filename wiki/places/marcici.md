# Marcici

Marcici is one of the most historically consequential cities in eastern Nereth. It served for centuries as the joint seat of both Emperor and Pontif during the [Veltric Empire](../history/veltric-empire.md), making it simultaneously the political and ecclesiastical center of the imperial order.

## Imperial role

Because both supreme offices were rooted in Marcici, the city concentrated an unusual degree of administrative, ceremonial, and legitimacy-bearing power. Imperial succession, ecclesiastical authority, and high politics all converged there.

## Role in the Fracture

Marcici became the focal point of the imperial collapse around **876 LC**, when the emperor died without an heir. In the aftermath, the Church in Marcici declared itself the de facto governing authority of the Empire until lawful continuity could be restored.

That declaration is widely treated as the public beginning of the [Fracture](../history/age-of-fracture.md). The city therefore matters not only as a former capital, but as the place where late-imperial succession crisis turned into enduring post-imperial rupture.

## Present position

Marcici now lies within the [Polbium Theocracy](../states/polbium-theocracy.md). Because the city still carries both imperial and ecclesiastical memory, control of Marcici remains central to claims of continuity with the old Veltric order.
