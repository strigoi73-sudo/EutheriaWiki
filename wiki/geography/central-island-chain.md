# Central Island Chain

The Central Island Chain is the island system that lies in the strait between [Valthera](valthera.md) and [Kasmora](kasmora.md). It is the single most important maritime chokepoint in Eutheria and one of the clearest examples in the setting of how geography can create outsized political power.

## Strategic role

The archipelago sits across the principal sea routes that connect the major inhabited continents through Oceanus Centralis. Ships moving between Valthera, Nereth, and Kasmora must either pass through waters shaped by the chain or accept far greater cost, danger, and inefficiency.

Because of this, the islands matter for more than simple naval basing. Control of the chain means leverage over trade, customs, convoy movement, military transit, smuggling, and the diplomatic terms under which maritime exchange takes place.

## Political control

Most of the chain is controlled by [Likia](../states/likia.md), whose naval supremacy allows it to regulate the strait and convert geographic position into lasting political influence.

Likian control is not merely nominal. It depends on fortified ports, patrol systems, anchorages, logistical infrastructure, and the ability to keep sea lanes functioning under Likian terms. The island chain is therefore not just territory. It is the material basis of a maritime order.

## Peripheral islands and shadow economy

Not every island is under equally firm authority. Some smaller islands remain outside tight Likian control and sustain pirate enclaves, smuggling ports, and other semi-clandestine maritime communities.

These spaces matter because chokepoints generate evasion as well as regulation. Wherever customs, convoy discipline, and strategic passage become important, shadow networks arise to exploit the margins. The Central Island Chain therefore supports both formal maritime order and the illicit economies that parasitize it.

## World significance

The Central Island Chain helps explain why relatively small states can matter at the same scale as much larger continental powers. Whoever controls these islands does not merely own land. They shape the flow of worlds.
