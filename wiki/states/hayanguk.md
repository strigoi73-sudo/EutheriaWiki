# Hayanguk

Hayanguk is a substantial inland Korean theocracy in western Valthera. It is best understood as a defensive buffer state: geographically between Korean, Chinese, and Dwarven spheres, and religiously centered on a frontier synthesis viewed with suspicion by neighbors.

![Political map crop of Hayanguk](../assets/maps/hayanguk-map.png)

## Religious foundation

Hayanguk's defining public tradition is **Tongj Apostatism**, a Korean reinterpretation of Dwarven religious inheritance blended with elements associated with the Witnesses of Dedeok.

This gives Hayanguk a distinct profile:

- not simply a Korean state with a local sect
- a polity whose governing identity is built around frontier religious synthesis

In regional perception, Tongj Apostatism is treated as apostasy by Dedeokan institutions and as heresy in stricter Dwarven doctrinal framing. Dwarven reactions are often more pragmatic than polemical, while sharper hostility comes from the Dedeokan side.

## Geography and settlement

Hayanguk occupies an inland position in the south-central western part of Valthera. It has no ports and no open maritime access, which sharply distinguishes it from the more coastal and outward-facing states around it.

Its position places it between [Cheonguk](cheonguk.md) to the west and northwest, [Guan Guo](guan-guo.md) to the north and east, and [Han Guo](han-guo.md) to the south. It also fronts the rougher upland belt associated with nearby Dwarven corridors, making it a hinge state between lowland and upland worlds.

## Economy and settlement

Hayanguk is economically solid, mostly rural, and inwardly oriented. With a population of roughly 2.53 million, it is a meaningful regional power, but not one organized around a dominant commercial metropolis.

Its economy relies on:

- agriculture and forestry
- river-linked internal transport
- trade with nearby Dwarven zones

It has no major maritime profile and no defining port system. Hayanguk is better understood as self-sufficient and locally connected than as a long-distance mercantile crossroads. Its burgs are culturally Korean, while its religious life is more mixed than a sealed sacred core, reinforcing its identity as a pressured frontier society rather than an isolated confessional enclosure.

## Political character

Hayanguk is a theocracy, but its most important political trait is its defensive mentality. It sees itself as a beleaguered state that has carved out a precarious religious space and must preserve the internal dominance of Tongj Apostatism against outside pressure.

## Diplomatic posture

Hayanguk is generally polite but reserved in external relations.

The state prioritizes internal religious order and treats unsupervised foreign influence as a potential threat to social cohesion. Outsiders are usually tolerated as visitors, traders, or envoys, but under clear local supervision.

## Fortification and regional role

Hayanguk is more fortified than average, reinforcing its identity as a defensive frontier polity rather than an expansionist theocracy. Its importance in western Valthera lies less in commerce or naval power than in its buffer role between Korean, Chinese, and Dwarven spheres.

## Regional context

Hayanguk sits inside a western Valtheran political field that also includes:

- [Han Guo](han-guo.md), a mixed Chinese-Korean republic
- [Cheonguk](cheonguk.md), the largest Korean monarchy in the region
- [Guan Guo](guan-guo.md), an orderly Chinese river-gate kingdom
- [Quz Guo](quz-guo.md), a sacred-sovereign Taizhouist center protected by Han Guo
