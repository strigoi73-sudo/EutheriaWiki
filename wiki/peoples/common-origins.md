# Common Origins

The humanoid peoples of Eutheria share a common ancient origin. Over very long periods, that ancestral stock diversified into the distinct lineages known today. This shared descent is one of the most important background truths in Eutherian anthropology, because it explains why apparently different peoples can still be understood within a single broad family rather than as unrelated creations.

The oldest major branches include [elves](elves.md), [dwarves](dwarves.md), [gnomes](gnomes.md), and [halflings](halflings.md). [Humans](humans.md) and [orcs](orcs.md) emerged later from an elven ancestral population, while half-elves and [half-orcs](half-orcs.md) arise through interbreeding between closely related branches. This makes the humanoid world historically diverse but biologically connected.

Common origin does not imply common development. Different lineages adapted to very different environments and historical pressures. Long lifespan, reproductive rate, diet, mobility, terrain preference, and social organization all pushed the branches of the humanoid family in different directions over time. That is why lineage in Eutheria is best understood as a starting point rather than a complete explanation of culture or history.
