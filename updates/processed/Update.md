## Changes
Use the following points as supported canon for this update:

- Haria is the principal Berber kingdom of Kasmora and the main political center of Berber civilization in the inhabited world.
- Haria is large and culturally cohesive; its identity should be grounded first in Berber civilizational centrality rather than reduced merely to trade or maritime advantage.
- Haria's dominant religion is Delistanism, but in Haria it is expressed in a non-theocratic, community-led form.
- Haria stands in useful contrast to Dukan: both are Delistanist, but Harian religious life is socially rooted rather than fused to coercive clerical rule.
- Haria occupies a long and strategically significant southeastern coastline in Kasmora.
- Haria has leverage over important southern sea routes, including waters through which Hawa must pass to reach Likia.
- Haria should be treated as a maritime actor with real regional influence, but its maritime importance grows out of its status as a major Berber kingdom rather than replacing it.
- In broad strokes, Haria is a culturally central, Delistanist, outward-facing coastal kingdom: cohesive, self-confident, and more strategically assertive than Har, while remaining distinct from theocratic Dukan.
