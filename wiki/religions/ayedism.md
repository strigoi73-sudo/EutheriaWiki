# Ayedism

Ayedism is a major organized polytheist tradition centered in Kasmora, with strong association to Arabic-analog religious culture and sacred geography rather than temple-centered monumental bureaucracy.

## Religious structure

Ayedist practice is strongly place-oriented. Pilgrimage routes, sacred landscapes, and ritual travel are as important as fixed clerical institutions.

Its holiest known site is [Tuwaid](../places/tuwaid.md) in [Jora Territory](../places/jora-territory.md), under the sovereignty of [Sala](../states/sala.md).

## Geographic distribution

Ayedism is dominant or highly significant in:

- [Sala](../states/sala.md)
- [Hawa](../states/hawa.md)
- parts of [Kordemeli](../states/kordemeli.md)

Kordemelian adoption is historically notable because it makes a major non-Arabic imperial polity dependent on sacred sites located in another kingdom.

## Political implications

Because Tuwaid lies within Sala, Sala controls core pilgrimage access logistics for external Ayedist populations. This grants Sala recurring soft-power leverage in regional diplomacy, especially in relations with Kordemeli.

Ayedism therefore operates simultaneously as:

- a religious tradition,
- a trans-state mobility system,
- and a strategic factor in interstate negotiation.
