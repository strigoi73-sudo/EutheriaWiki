## Canon Corrections and Stable Decisions

### Kasmora / Sala religion correction
- The encyclopedia claim that Sala's capital worships Dedeok is incorrect.
- All Dedeok presence in Kasmora has been removed.
- Sala's capital should no longer be described as Dedeok-aligned.
- Any older encyclopedia references to Dedeok in Kasmora are superseded by this correction.

### Marcici, Polbium, and the Fracture
- Polbium is the core of the old Veltric Empire.
- Marcici was the seat of both Emperor and Pontif.
- After the emperor's death, the Church declared itself the de facto governing body of the Empire.
- That declaration is the public act that initiated the Fracture.

### Phased 876–1026 LC structure
The 150 years between imperial collapse and campaign present should not be treated as empty time or simple stabilization. Use this phase structure:

1. **876–900 LC: Immediate Collapse**
   - succession crisis
   - provincial breakaways
   - church assertion of authority
   - civil and regional war

2. **900–950 LC: Wars of Settlement**
   - successor states consolidate
   - borders harden unevenly
   - legitimacy struggles continue
   - trade and church authority regionalize

3. **950–1000 LC: False Stabilization**
   - people begin to believe the worst has passed
   - trade networks adapt
   - successor regimes normalize
   - church structures survive, but not with universal authority

4. **1000–1026 LC: Deepening Fracture**
   - institutions weaken again
   - violence and unrest increase
   - deeper metaphysical disturbances become harder to dismiss
   - the world enters the campaign era as a second, deeper phase of breakdown rather than the immediate aftermath of collapse

## Tengc Empire — Stable Overview

### Identity
- Tengc considers itself the oldest of the modern Valtheran states.
- This may plausibly be true, or at minimum is believable in-world, because its political order preserves an archaic clan-based structure rather than a dense later monarchy or bureaucracy.
- Its imperial title reflects antiquity, prestige, and seniority more than administrative complexity or material strength.

### Geography and settlement pattern
- Tengc is a large northern Valtheran state whose meaningful life is concentrated in the southern and southeastern coastal belt.
- Much of its northern reach consists of cold frontier land, broken coasts, forest margins, islands, and sparsely inhabited or nearly empty territory.
- The empire's map is larger than its effective demographic and administrative core.
- The south and southeast contain the main settlements, routes, and economically useful land.

### Economy
- Fishing is the predominant industry.
- Tengc is best understood as a fishery-centered northern state rather than a rich agrarian empire.
- The southern forested zones likely provide the material base for the state: timber, pitch, tar, resin, fuel, and other products tied to ships, workshops, and coastal life.
- The far northern frozen regions should not be imagined as major sources of broad-based wealth.
- Northern claims are better understood as producing limited frontier resources at high cost: fisheries, marine products, furs, and other narrow extractive returns.
- In practice, the productive southern and southeastern belt supports a much larger prestige geography to the north.

### Naval character
- Do not infer Tengc's naval nature from generator type labels alone.
- Tengc's maritime capability is real but narrow.
- It is likely strong in coastal patrol, fishery protection, convoying, resupply, and difficult cold-water navigation.
- It should not be imagined as a first-rate blue-water hegemon.
- Its strength comes from hardened mariners, requisitionable working vessels, and familiarity with local waters rather than from massive treasury-backed fleet infrastructure.
- A useful shorthand: Tengc has an empire-of-fishermen navy rather than an empire's grand battle fleet.

### Political structure
- Tengc is an empire in title, but politically closer to a sparse confederated realm.
- It should not be imagined as a dense centralized bureaucracy.
- The emperor is better understood as a senior overlord above regional clan-based powers than as the absolute head of an intrusive state machine.
- Local life is governed lightly.
- Most of Tengc's stability comes from the fact that the center leaves distant regions alone unless larger obligations are at stake.

### Social structure
- Family clans choose their regional chiefs.
- Those regional chiefs in turn elect an emperor.
- The emperor serves for a fixed term of ten years.
- Succession is therefore elective rather than hereditary.
- The imperial title sits atop a layered clan-based order rather than replacing it.

### Notes for future expansion
- If future work deepens Tengc, keep it rugged, maritime, sparse, clan-mediated, and materially narrower than its map suggests.
- Do not drift toward a rich centralized northern superstate.

## Kaihui — Stable Overview

### Historical position
- Kaihui was formerly the Kingdom of Kaihui.
- Before the Likian–Xin War, Kaihui's prosperity depended heavily on the maritime order centered on Xin Guo.
- Kaihui was not a major naval power in its own right; Xin's ports and maritime capacity handled much of the outward movement of Kaihui's goods.
- During the war, Kaihui's participation was logistical more than ideological or naval: grain, livestock, and timber from Kaihui helped fuel Xin Guo's war effort.
- Likia considered its settlement with Kaihui lenient; Kaihui's elites do not.

### Geography
- Kaihui lies in eastern Valthera on the southeastern seaboard, facing the western waters of Oceanus Centralis.
- It lies south of Guan Guo, north of Kan Guo, east of Han Guo, and west and southwest of the Guan–Longlin–Tengc bay system.
- Kaihui has more coastline than Guan Guo, but lacks the developed port network needed to convert that coastline into comparable maritime leverage.
- The maps suggest coasts that could have supported more significant harbors, but they were never developed at scale because Xin Guo's maritime system made that unnecessary before the war.
- Kaihui's interior of rolling plains, pasture, and fertile valleys is more important than its coast. It is best understood as a plains state with access to the sea rather than as a port republic with a hinterland.

### Trade and the port problem
- Before the war, Kaihui traded largely through Xin ports.
- It still trades through those former Xin-linked ports where necessary, but those routes are heavily tariffed.
- Kaihui therefore prefers to move trade through Guan Guo's ports and through exchange with Kan Guo when possible.
- Kaihui is coastal without being maritime-dominant.

### Economy
- Kaihui is one of the major food-producing states of eastern Valthera.
- Vast rolling plains and fertile valleys are the primary source of foodstuffs for Kaihui and for important neighboring markets.
- Everyone depends too much on Kaihui's grain to casually upset the regional balance of power.
- At the same time, Kaihui's productivity makes it an obvious target for ambitious neighbors, especially Kan Guo.
- Agriculture, pastoralism, and horse breeding matter more to Kaihui than port trade.

### Horses and identity
- Kaihui hosts the largest population of Valtheran Thoroughbreds in the world.
- Horses are not just a resource; they are a core part of national identity.
- Kaihui's relationship to horses should be understood more like that of a horse-people society than like a simple noble pastime.
- Strict laws govern horse export.
- Large-scale foreign sale of horses is broadly opposed because it strengthens neighbors and threatens Kaihui's independence.
- Horse milk, including kumis-like fermented mare's milk, is a staple in Kaihui.

### Society
- Kaihui has a significant nomadic or semi-nomadic sub-population, particularly in the northern non-urban plains.
- The society is communal, conservative, and shaped by older horse-clan traditions.
- It values elders, local leaders, lineage, and deference to recognized authority.
- The government is not highly respected in its own right; many rural Kaihui communities have little direct need to engage deeply with the state.

### Government
- The Republic of Kaihui is publicly celebrated, especially among commoners, but much of the merchant class and aristocracy regard it as a foreign political system imposed by Likia.
- In practical terms it is a republic in name more than in sovereign function.
- The public face of the assembly is the Prytanarch.
- The common pejorative term for the Prytanarch is Kongzui, "empty mouth."
- Real executive power lies in the dual presidency: the Zuozheng and the Youzheng.
- These two offices can veto proposals from the assembly.
- Their terms are four years and are staggered every other year.
- The Zuozheng and Youzheng, together with the Prytanarch and the assembly of provincial governors, shape the pool of candidates from which the popular assembly votes.
- The bureaucracy descended from the old royal machinery remains the state's most durable governing organ.

### Religion
- Kaihui's traditional religion is the worship of the Xinchang Deities.
- Religion is treated as a personal issue rather than as a public identity.
- Temples and heavily anchored monuments are largely absent from Kaihui's visible cultural landscape.
- This should not be read as irreligion; it reflects a household, local, and family-centered mode of observance.
- This restrained religious pattern distinguishes Kaihui from Xin Guo, where the same religion is treated more as an obligatory social contract.

### Foreign posture
- Kaihui sees itself as protectionist, non-threatening, and conservative in its relations with neighbors.
- It is defensive rather than aggressive.
- The Likian war is not widely remembered among ordinary people as Kaihui's own great struggle.
- The consequences of the war remain a thorn in the side of the administration and elite political memory more than in the everyday consciousness of the average citizen.

### Political synthesis
Kaihui is best understood as a proud pastoral-plains state forced into an externally imposed republican shell after materially supporting Xin Guo's war effort. It remains coastal but under-ported, inwardly shaped by horse culture and food production, and outwardly constrained by a treaty order it publicly performs but never truly embraced.

## Kan Guo and Fuz Guo — Stable Decisions

### Kan Guo — broad political read
- Kan Guo should be treated as a relatively straightforward feudal monarchy.
- It is useful for Valthera to have at least one state in this regional cluster that is not structurally unusual.
- The current data supports reading Kan Guo as a competent hereditary kingdom rather than a republic, clan-confederation, or heavily eccentric court system.
- A simple crown-and-lords structure is appropriate: king, major hereditary nobles, lesser landed lords, and crown authority strongest in strategic royal centers.
- Kan Guo's simplicity is part of its value as a regional contrast to Kaihui and Tengc.
- Hejian is its political center.
- It should be understood more as a landed monarchy than as a naval identity.

### Fuz Guo inside Kan Guo
- The Duchy of Fuz Guo should be treated as an old semi-autonomous feudal duchy enclosed within Kan Guo.
- The cleanest explanation is historical rather than geometric: Fuz Guo is an old privileged holdout whose chartered rights survived later Kan consolidation.
- It may be understood as a marcher or allied ducal house that became enclosed by later royal acquisitions without losing its ancient privileges.
- Fuz Guo is not a fully foreign sovereign state in practice; it owes homage to the King of Kan Guo while retaining unusual hereditary rights, internal autonomy, and prestige.
- Zhang is its center.
- Its continued distinctness helps signal that Kan Guo is an old monarchy with at least one surviving feudal irregularity rather than a perfectly rationalized kingdom.

### Sorcerous nobility of Fuz Guo
- The nobility of Fuz Guo are sorcerous in the Pathfinder sense: their magical claim is blood-based and hereditary rather than learned in the manner of wizardly scholarship.
- The duchy's old prestige rests in part on the historic reputation of its noble houses as bearers of genuine sorcerous blood.
- In earlier centuries, that bloodline seems to have produced more formidable sorcerers than it does in the present.
- In the current era, the blood has been significantly diluted through time and intermarriage, and truly powerful sorcerers are now rare.
- Minor manifestations, weak gifts, unstable talents, or no obvious magical expression at all are now more common than major sorcerous power.
- Even so, the reputation of the old blood remains politically useful and is one reason the duchy continues to preserve its autonomy and prestige inside Kan Guo.
- Fuz Guo should not be treated as an overpowering mage-state. Its reputation is now stronger than its average magical reality.

### General rule for sorcerous bloodlines
- Sorcerous bloodlines in Eutheria may be inherited, but they cannot be reliably intensified or deliberately bred upward in a stable way.
- Such bloodlines are volatile, uneven in expression, and more easily diluted than cultivated.
- Families may preserve the possibility of sorcerous manifestation through careful marriage and genealogical conservatism, but they cannot force greatness to breed true.
- Sorcerous power may skip generations, appear unexpectedly in cadet branches, manifest unevenly among siblings, or fade almost to nothing in an otherwise prestigious line.
- This rule should be treated as general worldbuilding logic, not merely a peculiarity of Fuz Guo.

### Fuz Guo — concise synthesis
Fuz Guo is best understood as an old privileged duchy inside Kan Guo whose noble houses retain ancient claims to hereditary sorcerous blood. That blood once seems to have produced far stronger sorcerers than it does now, but its surviving prestige still helps preserve the duchy's autonomy within an otherwise straightforward feudal monarchy.

## See of Xin Guo — Stable Overview

### Historical frame
- The old Kingdom of Xin Guo was the great Valtheran naval rival to Likia.
- Likia defeated and dissolved the kingdom, prohibited naval restoration, and required that a new order replace it.
- Likia did **not** design the mature internal structure of the See.
- The current church-state was shaped afterward by Xin Guo's own surviving religious and administrative elites.
- The See should therefore be treated as an indigenous post-defeat clerical reconstruction, not merely a foreign-imposed constitutional shell.

### Geography and present condition
- The See lies in eastern Valthera on the southeastern seaboard, facing the western waters of Oceanus Centralis.
- It is a small insular-and-coastal remnant rather than a deep territorial state.
- Its geography remains strongly maritime in orientation, but it no longer has the scale or depth to function as a true naval power.
- It should be read as a sea-born remnant whose coast now supports communication, diplomacy, and exposure more than maritime dominance.

### Core political identity
- The See is a true church-state rather than a monarchy with priests layered on top.
- It is best understood as a humiliated maritime theocracy that reinvented itself as a clerical power of education, diplomacy, confessional influence, and intelligence.
- It channels the frustrated outward impulse of old Xin Guo into religion, doctrine, and soft power rather than fleets.

### Leadership structure
- The supreme hierarch of the See is the **Zhadrin**.
- The senior clerical council and bureaucratic governing body of the state is the **Khashung**.
- The See is ruled, when possible, by a true Cleric recognized as spiritually fit by the senior clergy.
- A true Cleric is necessary but not sufficient to become Zhadrin.
- If no true and spiritually fit Cleric can be recognized, authority reverts provisionally to the Khashung until one is acknowledged.
- These rules emerged after the See's creation as part of Xin's own internal development.

### Clergy and education
- Across much of eastern Valthera, clergy are generally the principal educators.
- This gives the See influence far beyond its own territory through schools, tutors, texts, and trained clergy.
- What clergy may teach is usually regulated by the state under which they are employed.
- The See's educational influence therefore varies by host society.
- Its soft power is strongest in courts, capitals, and literate urban settings.

### Diplomacy, intrigue, and intelligence
- The See wields substantial soft power despite its small territorial base.
- Its priest-diplomats are among the most distinguished in the world.
- It possesses an extensive intelligence network embedded across Xinchang capitals, burgs, clerical houses, educational institutions, and diplomatic channels.
- This network should be understood as growing organically out of correspondence, teaching, confessional ties, archives, missions, and diplomatic presence rather than as a wholly separate rogue apparatus.

### Religion and foreign Xinchang communities
- In the See itself, worship of the Xinchang Deities is treated as a public, socially organizing, and institutionally supervised order.
- The See seeks to make itself the arbiter of Xinchang orthodoxy abroad.
- It aims to turn private, household-centered, or merely local forms of Xinchang observance into more public and socially binding confessional systems.

### Kaihui-specific dynamic
- The See is currently trying to socialize the faith in Kaihui in order to gain influence in an otherwise stubbornly traditional population.
- It is largely failing.
- It has established some influence in Kaihui's capital through education, etiquette, elite language, and polite formal recognition.
- Outside those circles, Kaihui's household-centered religious life, low-literacy plains culture, and indifference to clerical socialization sharply limit the See's success.
- Kaihui gives the See courtesy and occasional lip service, not deep obedience.

### Tone
- The See should feel sincere and political at the same time.
- Its true high Clerics may be genuine paragons of Lawful Good devotion even while the wider ecclesiastical state beneath them operates through disciplined bureaucracy, diplomacy, and intrigue.
- Its ordinary clerical culture should trend more institutional and law-bound than saintly.

## Hidden History and Present Crisis (GM Layer)

### The Cult of the Void — true agenda
- The Cult worships the three Great Old Ones known to it.
- It does not seek normal worldly rule for its own sake.
- Its actual agenda is to weaken the structures of Law that make ordered existence resistant to the Kenoma.
- It seeks the end not only of kingdoms or worlds, but ultimately the unmaking of creation.
- The Cult is more threatened by strong lawful institutions than by good institutions as such, because Law sustains continuity, hierarchy, contracts, archives, rituals, succession, and enforceable meaning.

### Why the Cult targets the Empire and the Church
The Cult focuses on the two greatest lawful institutions of eastern Nereth:
1. **The Veltric Empire** — the greatest mortal engine of legal, administrative, and civic continuity.
2. **The Rocciaganel Church** — the greatest surviving institution of lawful spiritual interpretation, ritual continuity, and public legitimacy.

The Cult opposes them because they help reality remain socially coherent.

### Ancient precedent
- Roughly 11,000 years ago, the Drow and Hryngar were driven upward by pressures of unknown origin in the deepest underworld.
- The cleanest hidden explanation is that those pressures were the earliest known effects of void-contact in the deep world.
- This does **not** mean the modern Cult already existed. It means some earlier contact event, text-fragment, rite-complex, or proto-tradition linked to the same class of forbidden knowledge had already breached the world.

### The Thousand-Year War as a metaphysical crisis
- The Thousand-Year War remains, in overt history, a war between the Deep Folk and the surface civilizations that ended at Trelhelm in 9034 BLC.
- Hidden truth: the war was not only military and civilizational.
- Part of the deep-world destabilization behind it was tied to ancient void-pressure.
- The ancient world was stronger in high magic, dragons, saints, and spiritual power than the present world.
- The eventual turning of the tide likely involved not only military victory but the suppression or re-dormancy of the underlying void-channel that had intensified the crisis.

### Sequestration of forbidden knowledge
- After Trelhelm, dangerous void-related knowledge was likely seized, concealed, fragmented, or sealed by one or more preservationist powers.
- The elves are the cleanest primary candidates because of their long lifespans, magical sophistication, and preservationist role.
- The safest canon reading is that the elves or another postwar coalition suppressed an **older void witness-text, fragment tradition, recension, or ritual corpus** from which later derivative material resurfaced.

### The modern cult is new, the knowledge is old
- The modern Cult of the Void remains a late Veltric phenomenon.
- Approximately 150 years before the present age, a manuscript of profoundly disturbing origin came into mortal hands in the final era of the Veltric Empire.
- Its translators were broken by what they learned and founded the modern Cult.
- The Cult is therefore modern.
- The knowledge that birthed it is ancient.

### Church infiltration
- The Church is not secretly false or wholly cult-controlled.
- Most clergy remain sincere.
- The Cult's infiltration is best understood as a long project of placing, grooming, infecting, compromising, or subtly guiding individuals within the Church's most legitimacy-bearing organs: seminaries, doctrinal courts, archives, scriptoria, succession records, seal-keepers, and high clerical offices.
- The goal is to defile the religion from within so that an institution once dedicated to Law increasingly generates contradiction, mistrust, and fragmentation.

### The compromised Pontif
- By 1026 LC, one of the Cult's lines of influence has finally reached the top of the Church.
- The Pontif / supreme cleric is compromised.
- The strongest working model is that he is not necessarily a conscious cultist, but a damaged, manipulable thrall elevated and steered by lower-ranking cult-aligned clerics.
- This is current hidden fact and should remain GM-only unless deliberately surfaced.

### The Veltric collapse reframed
- Publicly, the collapse begins when the emperor dies without an heir around 876 LC.
- Hidden truth: the Cult's objective was not simply puppet rule.
- Its aim was to break the Empire's capacity for lawful continuity at the exact moment renewed deep pressure was beginning to rise.
- It sought to ensure that archives, church recognition, and the imperial center could no longer produce uncontested legitimacy.
- The Church's declaration of rule then triggered the Fracture in public history while also serving the deeper logic of fragmentation.

### Deep Folk and the renewed pressure from below
- The Cult is not best understood as directly commanding the Deep Folk.
- The stronger model is that it is reactivating the same class of deep-world pressure that once destabilized them.
- The Drow and Hryngar retain their own civilizational agency.
- Renewed void-pressure below makes conflict, expansion, factional struggle, and upward pressure more likely without reducing the Deep Folk to puppets.

### High-level divine scarcity and existential stakes
- In the present age, truly apex lawful clerics are vanishingly rare.
- A good working assumption is that there may be only 0–1 living LG level 18 clerics in the entire world.
- Even level 18 clerics in general should be extremely rare.
- The Great Old Ones must remain a legitimate existential threat to the entire metaverse.
- If all three known Great Old Ones awaken at once, mortals are unlikely to be sufficient by themselves.
- Dragons, powerful outsiders, and other ancient defenders of ordered existence may have to intervene.
- This should remain part of the setting's hidden escalation model rather than common public understanding.

## Working principle for future integration

The single best shorthand for the current hidden-history model is:

**The modern Cult of the Void is new; the void knowledge that birthed it is ancient.**

And the single best shorthand for the current campaign-era crisis is:

**The world is not merely living 150 years after the imperial collapse. It is entering a second, deeper phase of fracture in which older void-pressure is returning to a weaker and more divided world.**
