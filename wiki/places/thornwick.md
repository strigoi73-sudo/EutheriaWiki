# Thornwick

Thornwick is the default starting settlement within [Dudbury](../geography/dudbury.md), a substantial parish-green village in inland [Lienia](../states/lienia.md). It is not a burg or true town, but the dominant local village-center within its immediate district.

## Settlement class and appearance

Thornwick is best understood as a head-village or parish-service settlement of roughly 350 people. It gathers worship, repair, storage, gossip, and customary authority without becoming urban.

Its physical character should be that of a village on a green rather than a paved square. The green, parish church, modest hall or manorial close, inn, smithy, mill, and a road-ribbon of cottages and sheds give it its local form. It should feel muddy, useful, lived-in, and socially legible rather than picturesque or fortified.

## District role

Thornwick serves a wider ring of farms, crofts, shepherd holdings, wood-edge households, and dependent hamlets. People come there for worship, milling, smithing and repairs, ale and lodging, local exchange and hiring, road news, and disputes too large for a hamlet to settle alone.

It is the only settlement in its class in the immediate local area. Nearby places are dependents rather than peers. Stable examples include **Hallowford**, **Alderfield**, **Stonebrook**, and **Olding Crossing**.

## District geography

Thornwick sits in an old, settled agricultural district rather than at the edge of blank wilderness. The immediate country around it should read as an open cultivated core of fields, lanes, pasture, and parish land, broken by low ridges, brooks, smaller woods, and rougher margins.

The district should feel long-settled, named, and socially familiar, with older site-memory layered across ordinary working land.

## Threat structure

Thornwick's danger profile is civilized-center and dangerous-margin, not direct wilderness siege. The open core around the village is worked and ordered; pressure comes from the edge-ring of larger woods, broken ridges, abandoned workings, old burial or shrine landscapes, forgotten paths, and under-patrolled seams.

Three named pressure-zones matter especially:

- **Duskmire Wood**, the likeliest center of recurring goblin pressure
- **Old Quarry**, an abandoned working and secondary danger-node
- **Barrow Heath**, an older memorial and burial landscape where danger attaches to taboo and old ground

## St. Cuthbert's Well

**St. Cuthbert's Well** is not a true well but a natural fissure leading into a wider cave system that local tradition says eventually opens into the Underrealm. The name survives from local mockery rather than formal sanctity: **Cuthbert** was a hermit woman once called a saint in derision for her zeal and strangeness, then later fell into the fissure and died there.

Her ruined homestead still survives nearby as little more than a foundation and chimney. The site is now deeply taboo, and local adults still joke darkly about throwing wayward children to St. Cuthbert, but the humor should be treated as black folk speech around a genuinely feared place rather than as casual irreverence.

## Summary

Thornwick is an old parish-green head-village in a settled Dudbury district: the only true local center in a mesh of dependent hamlets and old named sites, secure enough at its core to feel civilized, but ringed by woods, ruins, fissures, and forgotten ground where danger still persists.
