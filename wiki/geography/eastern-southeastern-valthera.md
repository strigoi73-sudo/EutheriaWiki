# Eastern and Southeastern Valthera

Eastern and southeastern Valthera are best understood as a connected littoral system rather than as a scattering of isolated states. Their present shape was forged by the destruction of Xin Guo, the long cold peace with Likia, and the uneven control of plains, ports, and narrow coastal outlets.

## Regional character

This is the most intricate political cluster in present-day Valthera. Treaty corridors, clerical influence, remembered war, and practical water-control matter as much as raw land area.

No state in the region can be understood without reference to its waters. Plains wealth, sheltered transport corridors, outlet control, and maritime dependence all shape the balance.

## Major states and roles

- [Kaihui](../states/kaihui.md): plains-and-horse republic, indispensable for grain and vulnerable because of it
- [Kan Guo](../states/kan-guo.md): the clearest feudal hard-power monarchy in the cluster
- [The Marches of Kai Guo](../states/kai-guo.md): treaty-managed littoral march controlling a southern watershed outlet
- [Yan](../states/yan.md): minor but durable fishing-and-harbor duchy
- [See of Xin Guo](../states/xin-guo.md): small clerical remnant with outsized soft power
- [Tengc](../states/tengc.md): sparse northern fishery-confederation whose claims run beyond its productive core

## Religious and political gradient

The See of Xin Guo attempts to shape the wider Xinchang world, but success varies sharply by state. Kaihui resists deeper clerical socialization outside elite circles, Yan remains conventionally Xinchang without enthusiasm for See control, and Kan Guo appears stable enough that See influence functions more as prestige than as shared rule.

## Practical geography

Kaihui's plains and valleys generate food, horses, and internal strength without producing full maritime autonomy. Kan Guo's monarchy is more directly actionable and benefits from a more usable coastal posture. The Marches of Kai Guo matter because they control one strategically sensitive outlet rather than because they are large.

## Regional summary

Eastern and southeastern Valthera form a balance-of-systems littoral order defined by interdependence, grievance, clerical influence, guarded corridors, and remembered war.
