# Wiki Editorial Style Guide

This guide defines the default editorial rules for structural cleanup and future wiki maintenance.

## Ordering rules

- Alphabetize section index lists by article title unless the page is explicitly chronological.
- Alphabetize `Related` sections by linked article title unless chronological sequence is materially more useful.
- Use chronology for timeline pages, era pages, war sequences, and history hubs that are meant to be read as a sequence.
- Do not use mixed or ad hoc ordering inside a single list.

## Related sections

- Keep `Related` sections concise, relevant, and non-redundant.
- Prefer 2 to 8 links that materially help a reader continue browsing.
- Link to adjacent topics, supporting reference pages, or immediate neighboring regions and states.
- Do not use `Related` sections as a full index.
- Public pages must not link directly to GM-only pages.

## Common article structures

### States

- Lead: concise identity, scale, and strategic role.
- Geography or structural position.
- Politics, society, religion, or economy as needed.
- History only when material to current understanding.
- `Related`.

### Geography and regions

- Lead: scale and defining role.
- Physical or structural geography.
- Settlement, economy, or strategic significance as needed.
- Regional subdivisions or internal logic where helpful.
- `Related`.

### History

- Lead: what period, event, or process the page covers.
- Chronological subsections where useful.
- Keep causal explanation clear without overloading the page.
- Use chronology-ordered lists only where sequence is the point.
- `Related`.

### Religions, cultures, peoples, and cosmology

- Lead with the clearest defining idea first.
- Move from identity to structure to influence or significance.
- Keep scope tight and article-focused.
- `Related`.

### GM pages

- State hidden truths directly and analytically.
- Prefer clarity over in-world tone.
- GM pages may link to public pages, but not the reverse.

## Prose rules

- Use a short encyclopedic lead.
- Preserve canon nuance and intended uncertainty.
- Avoid repetition, filler transitions, and unnecessary restatement.
- Keep cross-links purposeful rather than exhaustive.
- Preserve established terminology from canon sources.
- Do not invent unsupported facts.

## Review checklist

- Ordering follows either alphabetical or clear chronological logic.
- `Related` links are useful, concise, and non-redundant.
- Public pages do not link to GM-only pages.
- Revised pages remain readable and encyclopedic.
- No broken internal links were introduced.
