# Humanoid Lineages

The humanoid lineages of Eutheria descend from a shared ancient stock but differ sharply in lifespan, adaptation, ecology, and historical role. Their differences are the result of long divergence within a common family rather than of wholly separate origins.

## Major lineages

- [Humans](humans.md) are the most numerous and adaptable, with rapid reproduction and wide geographic spread.
- [Elves](elves.md) are among the oldest surviving lineages, long-lived and strongly associated with wilderness and magic.
- [Dwarves](dwarves.md) are ancient, durable, and deeply tied to highland, mountain, and crafted environments.
- [Gnomes](gnomes.md) and [halflings](halflings.md) represent smaller branches of the broader humanoid family with their own long historical development.
- [Orcs](orcs.md) emerged later from an elven ancestral branch, while [half-orcs](half-orcs.md) arise where closely related lineages intermingle.
- [Drow](drow.md) and [hryngar](hryngar.md) represent underworld-adapted peoples whose histories became entangled with the Deep Folk crisis.

## Divergence and adaptation

Lineage helps explain broad capacities and tendencies, but it does not determine political or cultural life by itself. Elven longevity encourages continuity and long memory, dwarven mountain adaptation supports fortified craft societies, and human adaptability helps explain their demographic spread. Orc, drow, and hryngar development also shows how environment and long conflict can shape a people as strongly as biological descent.

The lineage model is therefore most useful when paired with [history](../history/index.md) and [geography](../geography/index.md). Peoples do not merely descend; they settle, disperse, fight, adapt, and remember.
