# Tongj Apostates

The Tongj Apostates are a Valtheran religious tradition associated with frontier contact between Korean-analog and Dwarven populations, especially in upland regions connected to [Hayanguk](../states/hayanguk.md).

Current canon treats the tradition as evidence that Valthera's mountain frontiers are active zones of exchange and reinterpretation, not hard civilizational borders.

## Canonical significance

The Tongj Apostates matter less as a mass-conversion imperial religion and more as a marker of contact-driven religious development. Their existence supports a broader reading of Valthera in which faith, culture, and political identity are repeatedly reshaped in frontier environments.

## Current limitations

Many details remain open in encyclopedia-level canon, including:

- formal doctrine and liturgical structure
- clerical hierarchy and institutional centers
- geographic extent beyond Hayanguk-linked frontier zones
- relations with larger regional religious traditions
