# Yan

Yan is a small Tengcian Xinchang coastal duchy whose survival has depended less on force than on caution, cohesion, and a careful awareness of its own limits.

![Political map crop of Yan](../assets/maps/yan-map.png)

## Historical tone

The ducal house presents itself domestically as the surviving heir of an older lost kingdom, but in foreign affairs it behaves with marked restraint. That contrast explains much of Yan's political personality.

## Geography and economy

Yan is a shore duchy rather than a maritime power. Its life is organized around one fortified harbor capital and a modest inland settlement spine, with an economy based on fishing, harbor service, small-scale coastal shipping, and local agriculture.

## Public life

Yan is conventionally Xinchang in religion and generally cohesive in tone. It stands between Kaihui's more household-centered religious style and the See of Xin Guo's more assertive clerical order, remaining content with a local and inherited public culture.
