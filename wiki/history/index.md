# History

Eutherian history is a long sequence of rise, devastation, recovery, consolidation, and renewed fracture. The setting's present is not a settled post-collapse equilibrium, but the latest phase in a much older process whose roots reach back to the age of dragons, the fall of giant civilizations, the Great Catastrophe, and the eventual breakdown of Veltric imperial order. For a concise orientation, begin with the [Summary Timeline](summary-timeline.md), then expand into the major era articles below.

## Deep Past

The oldest recoverable history belongs to the pre-humanoid world. [Pre-Humanoid World](pre-humanoid-world.md) describes the age of dragons, the rise of reptilian sacred complexes, the emergence of proto-humanoids, and the later age of giants. This is the layer of history that explains why the world contains such different archaeological strata, and why some of its oldest ruins are only imperfectly understood even by learned scholars.

The [Geological Timeline](geological-timeline.md) complements that narrative by showing how tectonic change, the breakup of older continental formations, and the long Ice Age created the physical conditions inherited by later peoples. The [Archaeological Landscape](archaeological-landscape.md) is especially important because it explains why Eutherian history survives as overlapping layers of ruin rather than as a single clean civilizational record.

## Catastrophe and Recovery

The central rupture of ancient humanoid history is the [Great Catastrophe and the Thousand-Year War](great-catastrophe-and-the-thousand-year-war.md). That conflict ended at Trelhelm just as the Great Withdrawal began, meaning the exhausted survivors of the war entered a world whose geography was itself changing around them. In the long aftermath, old powers collapsed, populations dispersed, and recovery proceeded unevenly across the continents.

This asymmetrical recovery matters. Kasmora regained stable agricultural life earlier than the northern continents, while Nereth and Valthera emerged more slowly from dark-age conditions into clusters of kingdoms, cultic centers, and regional polities. The result was a world in which later states inherited not a uniform starting point, but very different historical burdens and opportunities depending on region.

## Empire and Fracture

The greatest later land power was the [Veltric Empire and the Age of Fracture](veltric-empire-and-age-of-fracture.md). Veltric rule brought roads, law, administration, and a durable political center to eastern Nereth, but its collapse did not simply clear the field for stable successor states. Instead, the imperial death and the church's claim to de facto governing authority initiated the Fracture, which remains the defining political condition of much of the modern world.

The modern era is therefore best understood through unresolved inheritance. [Present Day (1026 LC)](present-day-1026-lc.md) presents the current fractured landscape, while [Likia and the Valtheran Naval Conflict](likia-and-the-valtheran-naval-conflict.md) shows how post-imperial conditions interact with maritime power, trade realignment, and regional rivalry. In Eutheria, history is rarely over. It continues to act through borders, institutions, religious authority, and the memory of lost orders.
