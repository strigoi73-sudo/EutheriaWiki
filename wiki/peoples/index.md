# Peoples

The peoples of Eutheria share deep ancestry, but that common origin has diversified over immense time into very different species, lifeways, and historical roles. Biology matters, but it does not operate alone. Environment, migration, war, isolation, adaptation, and long civilizational memory all shape how a people appears in the world and how later cultures interpret them.

## Common Origins and Divergence

[Common Origins](common-origins.md) provides the basic framework: the humanoid peoples descend from a shared ancient lineage that later diversified into the species now known across the world. [Humanoid Lineages](humanoid-lineages.md) expands that frame by clarifying the branching relationship between elves, dwarves, gnomes, halflings, humans, and orcs, as well as the hybrid peoples that can arise where closely related branches remain biologically compatible.

This common origin matters because it explains both deep kinship and later divergence. Humans became numerous and adaptable across multiple continents; elves retained longer time horizons and lower-density settlement patterns; dwarves specialized in mountain strongholds and long-duration craft cultures. None of these developments can be understood only as biological difference. Each is also a historical adaptation to place and circumstance.

## Peoples and Historical Role

The section is especially useful when read against the longer history of the world. The [Deep Folk](deep-folk.md) are not simply another people among many, but a category with major historical significance because of their role in the Thousand-Year War and the continuing memory of underworld pressure. [Dragons](dragons.md) stand partly outside ordinary humanoid history altogether, as the oldest mortal creatures and a lineage whose significance is as much cosmological as political.

For campaign use, this section works best as a broad orientation rather than a complete social atlas. It helps explain who the major peoples are, where they came from, and why some of them occupy such different positions in myth, ecology, and history. Readers looking for lived social expression should continue into [Cultures](../cultures/index.md), [States](../states/index.md), and [Geography](../geography/index.md), where those lineages become concrete societies.
