# Kordemeli

Kordemeli is a large northern khaganate in Kasmora and one of the continent's major land powers. It is politically Turkish in character but culturally composite, ruling a broad imperial space that includes Likian subject populations alongside Ayedist and Skrosenist communities.

![Political map crop of Kordemeli](../assets/maps/kordemeli-map.png)

## Political character

Kordemeli is durable because it governs pragmatically. Its authority rests on hierarchy, military command, and loyalty to the Khagan, but it does not attempt to erase every local system it absorbs.

This gives the khaganate a distinctly imperial form of strength. It can remain cohesive over wide territory because it treats submission and order as more important than total uniformity.

## Governance model

Communities that submit and keep the peace often retain local laws, religions, and institutions. This is not liberalism in the modern sense; it is an imperial practicality that reduces resistance and lowers the administrative cost of rule.

In former Likian areas, that model is especially visible. Some cities preserve municipal autonomy, while ports remain under more direct military and customs supervision. Kordemeli is therefore not simply a conquering state, but a layered one, capable of mixing indirect and direct rule according to strategic need.

Much of this western Likian belt was absorbed during the later Likian-Xin Guo rivalry period, when Likia's eastern maritime commitments left western provinces politically exposed. In several cities, local elites accepted Kordemelian supremacy in exchange for protection and civic continuity.

## Regional relationships

Kordemeli maintains warm ties with [Sala](sala.md), relying on Salan grain and overland connections strengthened by pilgrimage movement and trade.

Its relationship with [Likia](likia.md) is one of long-term strategic tension without outright decisive war. Each limits the other in a different domain: Kordemeli on land, Likia at sea.

## Strategic significance

Kordemeli matters because it anchors the northern continental balance in Kasmora. It is large enough to be feared, pragmatic enough to endure, and plural enough to avoid the self-destruction that often follows rigid homogenizing empire.

Its strength does not come from cultural simplicity, but from its ability to rule difference without dissolving into it.
