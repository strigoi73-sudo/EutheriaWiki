# Kaihui

Kaihui is a Tengcian republic in formal law, but in political reality it remains the heir to an older pastoral kingdom whose governing habits were never fully broken. It is a coastal-plains state whose strength lies in food production, livestock, horse culture, and treaty-shaped adaptation after the Likian-Xin war.

![Political map crop of Kaihui](../assets/maps/kaihui-map.png)

## Historical development

Before the war, Kaihui was a kingdom closely tied to Xin Guo's maritime order. After Likian victory, its monarchy was abolished and republican institutions were imposed by treaty. Its elites have never entirely reconciled themselves to that constitutional shell.

## Character

Kaihui is neither a true maritime power nor a purely inland realm. Its coasts matter, but its wealth remains grounded more in plains, valleys, and horse-breeding districts than in fleets.
