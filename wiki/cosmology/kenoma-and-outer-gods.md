# The Kenoma and the Outer Gods

The **Kenoma** is the metaphysical condition of incompleteness against which ordered existence appears as an anomaly. It is not simply destruction, corruption, or death within the ordinary cosmic system. It is the possibility of unmaking the system itself.

This distinction is essential. Many destructive things exist within the metaverse, including undeath, entropy, and negative energy. The Kenoma is more radical than any of them because it does not merely damage beings inside reality. It threatens the substrate that makes ordered reality possible at all.

## Relation to the cosmic order

The Kenoma stands outside the normal structure established in the [First Moment](first-moment.md). It is not one more hostile force within the planes. It is the metaphysical outside of the ordered world.

For that reason, encounters with the Kenoma or its expressions are not simply battles against monstrous power. They are confrontations with the instability of existence itself.

## Outer Gods

Outer Gods are native expressions of the Kenoma. They are not conventional beings, cannot be reasoned with in ordinary theological or diplomatic terms, and are not treated as normal statted entities.

This is not because they are merely very powerful monsters. It is because they do not belong to the same ontological category as creatures fully inside the ordered system.

## Great Old Ones

Great Old Ones are localized crystallizations of Outer God pressure within ordered existence. Unlike the Outer Gods themselves, they can be encountered in ways that make bestiary or game representation possible, though mortal understanding of them is often fragmentary, contradictory, and distorted.

They are therefore best understood as points where the pressure of the Kenoma becomes partially legible without becoming safe, normal, or fully containable.

## Cosmological significance

The Kenoma is important because it defines the outer limit of what the ordered world is not. It clarifies why negative energy, undeath, and other terrible forces are still part of the normal cosmic system, while the Outer Gods belong to a deeper category of threat altogether.

In practical terms, the Kenoma gives Eutherian cosmology a true exterior: not another plane, but the possibility that the whole architecture of planes and souls is contingent, vulnerable, and opposed by something that cannot be assimilated into ordinary existence.
