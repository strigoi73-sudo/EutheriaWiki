# Cultures

Culture in Eutheria operates at multiple scales. Some identities are civilizational and span multiple states; others are regional, urban, tribal, linguistic, or religious. Political borders matter, but they do not fully explain how people understand themselves.

This section is intended to document the social worlds that make the setting feel lived in: customs, kinship structures, education, religious practice, attitudes toward law and authority, material aesthetics, and the ways cultures change through trade, conquest, and coexistence.

## Topics this section will cover

- major civilizational cultures and their regional branches
- everyday social practice, not only elite ideology
- frontier and mixed-contact cultures
- the relationship between language, religion, and political identity
- cultural continuity after imperial collapse or migration

As the section develops, it will connect closely with the articles on states, peoples, religions, and geography.
