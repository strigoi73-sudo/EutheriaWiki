# Pre-Humanoid Ages

The deepest historical strata of Eutheria predate recorded humanoid civilization by immense spans of time. Canon identifies a sequence of pre-humanoid eras that explain later ruin distribution, mythic memory, and the long ecological background of the modern world.

## Age of Dragons

Before humanoid polities, dragons were the dominant intelligent lineage on the Material Plane. They are described as descendants of proto-draconic ancestors that developed powerful innate magic and sentience over long periods of astral exposure.

In canonical metaphysics, dragon souls are tightly alignment-bound; at death, they transfigure into corresponding outsider forms. This has major implications for early outsider origins in [cosmological history](../cosmology/kenoma-and-outer-gods.md).

## Reptilian civilizations

Following and overlapping draconic predominance, large reptilian civilizations flourished in multiple regions, especially in tropical and equatorial belts less disrupted by later glaciation. Their remains include temple complexes and ritualized monumental architecture linked to draconic cultic systems.

## Proto-humanoid divergence and the giant age

A proto-humanoid lineage later divided into:

- branches associated with giant-scale morphologies, and
- smaller-bodied branches associated with early elves and later humanoid diversification.

Giants then rose as major civilizational actors, constructing cyclopean monuments, fortified complexes, and large engineered corridors that persist as some of the oldest visible structures in the world.

## Dragon-Giant Wars

Dragon and giant competition escalated into prolonged, destructive conflict. The resulting mutual devastation produced a large civilizational vacuum. Smaller humanoid peoples—especially early elven polities—expanded into that space and became principal surface actors in subsequent eras.

## Archaeological legacy

Pre-humanoid and early post-humanoid layers produce a stacked archaeological landscape:

1. rare draconic-age survivals,
2. reptilian ritual complexes,
3. giant megastructural remains,
4. later humanoid strata.

Glaciation strongly affected survivorship: older layers are comparatively more visible in southern and tropical zones.
