# Dwarves of Valthera and Nereth

The dwarves of [Valthera](../geography/valthera.md) and [Nereth](../geography/nereth.md) are highland peoples whose historical importance comes from control of mountain corridors, fortified settlements, and difficult interior routes.

## Overview

Dwarven communities are concentrated in upland and pass-dominating zones where terrain rewards defensive depth and route expertise. Their significance is therefore strategic as well as cultural.

## Distribution

In both Valthera and Nereth, dwarven populations are distributed through mountain systems that connect and separate major regional economies. This position often places them between larger lowland powers.

## Historical distinctiveness

Dwarven political leverage has frequently come from controlling access rather than projecting broad territorial conquest. Their settlements shape commerce, military movement, and diplomatic bargaining at key continental choke corridors.

## Geography and state interaction

State powers attempt to incorporate or negotiate with dwarven communities because pass access cannot be ignored. Even when formally subordinated, mountain communities can retain practical autonomy through terrain and institutional cohesion.
