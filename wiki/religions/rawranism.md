# Rawranism

Rawranism is a fellowship-centered religion that developed within the Sipharan sphere from earlier Mesopotamian animist traditions.

## Core orientation

Rawranism emphasizes ordered community, moral cultivation, disciplined conduct, and trustworthy administration.

It is better understood as a civilization-shaping tradition than as a missionary expansion faith. In social function, it aligns most strongly with education, archives, councils, adjudication, and initiatory belonging.

## Force and politics

Rawranism is not absolute pacifism. It permits restrained force for preserving order but does not prioritize conquest.

The tradition is compatible with multiple constitutional forms. It underpins republican institutions in [Commonwealth of Sipharan](../states/sipharan.md) and also supports monarchical rule in [Kingdom of Har](../states/har.md).

## Civilizational role

Rawranism is associated with high literacy, legal scholarship, record-keeping, and civic formation. Its institutional culture helps explain Sipharan neutrality and Haran administrative conservatism.
