# History

This section traces Eutherian history from the pre-humanoid world through the Great Catastrophe, the Veltric age, and the fractured present.

Readers who want the clearest overview should begin with the [Summary Timeline](summary-timeline.md), then move outward into the major era articles. The [Great Catastrophe and the Thousand-Year War](great-catastrophe-and-the-thousand-year-war.md) explains the central civilizational rupture of the distant past, while the [Veltric Empire and the Age of Fracture](veltric-empire-and-age-of-fracture.md) shows how the collapse of imperial order still defines the political present.

The earlier articles in this section establish the depth of Eutheria's past rather than just its known chronology. [Pre-Humanoid World](pre-humanoid-world.md), the [Geological Timeline](geological-timeline.md), and the [Archaeological Landscape](archaeological-landscape.md) are especially useful for understanding why so much of the world's oldest history survives only in fragmentary form. [Present Day (1026 LC)](present-day-1026-lc.md) and [Likia and the Valtheran Naval Conflict](likia-and-the-valtheran-naval-conflict.md) bring that long arc back into campaign-era relevance.
