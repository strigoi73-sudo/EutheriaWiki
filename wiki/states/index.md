# States

The political map of Eutheria is highly varied, but it is not random. States are shaped above all by geography, inherited civilizational patterns, religious legitimacy, and the still-unsettled consequences of older collapses and wars. Some are dense agrarian monarchies, some are republics whose public forms conceal oligarchic realities, some are church-states or sacred polities, and others survive because they control a river gate, a coastal outlet, or a mountain-framed frontier.

## A Fragmented Political Order

No single political logic governs the whole world. Eastern Nereth still lives in the shadow of imperial collapse and the [Age of Fracture](../history/veltric-empire-and-age-of-fracture.md), while western Nereth is organized through maritime kingdoms, republics, upland monarchies, and transfer states with older regional histories of their own. Kasmora is populous and agriculturally rich but has resisted continental consolidation, producing a durable mosaic of kingdoms, theocracies, confederated societies, and littoral powers. Valthera, meanwhile, contains a particularly varied mix of republics, kingdoms, theocracies, march polities, and ritual states shaped by both geography and the aftereffects of the Likian-Xin conflict.

This means the modern world is not best read as a list of sovereign entities alone. It is a map of unresolved balances. Some states are large because their landscapes support them; others matter because their position makes them difficult to ignore. A small polity can be regionally indispensable if it guards a sacred center, a harbor system, or a neutral transit corridor.

## Major Geopolitical Patterns

Several broad patterns recur across the world. Maritime leverage remains decisive, which is why [Likia](likia.md) can matter far beyond its territorial scale and why coastal powers in Valthera and western Nereth remain so sensitive to sea access. Agrarian density also matters, producing states such as [Kan Guo](kan-guo.md), [Sala](sala.md), and the [Kordemelian Khaganate](kordemeli.md), whose weight comes from population, food production, and internal depth rather than from a single strategic trick.

Religion is equally important to political structure. [Polbium Theocracy](polbium-theocracy.md), the [Dukanese Theocracy](dukan.md), the [See of Xin Guo](xin-guo.md), and [Hayanguk](hayanguk.md) show different ways sacred authority can organize public life, sometimes as full theocracy and sometimes as a more defensive confessional order. Elsewhere, religion supports legitimacy without wholly defining the state, as in [Lienia](lienia.md), [Cheonguk](cheonguk.md), or [Kaihui](kaihui.md).

## Regional Diversity

The range of political forms is especially visible when comparing neighboring states. [Han Guo](han-guo.md) is a negotiated republican union, [Guan Guo](guan-guo.md) a river-gate kingdom, [Marches of Kai Guo](kai-guo.md) a treaty-managed littoral polity, and [Yan](yan.md) a small hereditary duchy that survives through caution rather than force. In western Nereth, [Sinz](sinz.md) and [Lienia](lienia.md) embody rival western power models, while [Fresen](fresen.md), [Bramia](bramia.md), [Garka](garka.md), and [Laups](laups.md) preserve older or more regionally specific political traditions.

For that reason, state articles are among the best campaign-facing entry points in the wiki. They gather geography, history, religion, society, and diplomacy into a single frame. Where source maps support it, major state pages also include cropped regional maps so readers can place each polity in its immediate strategic context rather than treating the world as an abstract list of names.
