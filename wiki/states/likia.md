# Likia

Likia is the maritime power of the known world: a Likian-culture naval state of Kasmoran coastal origin whose control of the Central Island Chain gives it unusual leverage over intercontinental passage.

![Political map crop of Likia](../assets/maps/likia-map.png)

## Origins and identity

Likian culture emerged from centuries of intermingling between Flandric seafarers and Kordemelian coastal peoples. The result is a genuinely distinct civilization rather than a simple extension of either parent culture. Founded at 0 LC on Kasmora's northeastern coast, Likia developed as a seaward-facing society from the beginning.

The state is formally a principality and publicly a democracy, but in practice it functions as an oligarchic commercial republic shaped by guild power, maritime interests, and the lingering influence of the three founding houses. Its social character is deeply marked by shipping, exchange, and a sense that sea power is the natural basis of national survival.

## Geography and power

Likia's core strength lies in the [Central Island Chain](../geography/central-island-chain.md) and the Kasmoran coastal territory that supports it. This gives the state leverage over the routes connecting Valthera, Nereth, and Kasmora. The island capital of Margaros, the old city of Likia on the coast, and the string of associated ports and supply settlements together form a political geography built around movement, naval reach, and chokepoint control.

Its power is therefore naval, commercial, and positional rather than primarily agrarian.

## Politics and relationships

Likia is commercially dominant, politically self-confident, and highly attentive to the balance of maritime power. Its most consequential historical conflict was the long naval rivalry that ended with the destruction of old Xin Guo and the creation of the [See of Xin Guo](xin-guo.md). In the present, its politics are shaped by three broad factions: hawkish democrats tied to military prestige and lost Likian communities under foreign rule, constitutionalists who dominate the machinery of government, and mercantilists who prize new trade frontiers and commercial integration.

Its key external relationships reflect that structure. [Lienia](lienia.md) is its most important western commercial partner, [Kordemelian Khaganate](kordemeli.md) its unavoidable continental neighbor, and the cold peace with Valthera remains one of the central facts of intercontinental politics. Religiously, Likia remains strongly [Skrosenist](../religions/skrosenism.md), though its version of the faith is more rationalist and maritime than many Flandric mainland forms.
