# Han Guo

Han Guo is a Chinese-classed republic in Valthera that is, in practice, a deeply integrated Chinese-Korean union.

Its official institutions, court language, and civil traditions are predominantly Chinese, but its social fabric is mixed and cohesive, with significant Chinese-Korean overlap and likely high bilingualism in urban and governing life.

## Political character

Han Guo is best understood as a negotiated republican compact rather than a conquest state. Current canon places its coherent republican consolidation in the post-Xin Guo order, when regional Chinese and Korean communities had strong incentives to pool resources under shared institutional forms.

## Capital and constitutional meaning

The capital, **Insan**, is Korean. In canon interpretation, this is not an anomaly but evidence of founding compromise: a Chinese-ordered republic governing from a Korean city, implying that both components of the union are foundational.

## Strategic and economic profile

Han Guo's northwestern holdings are treated as remote extraction outposts near the habitable fringe beneath the glacier, not as a second demographic heartland.

Its broader legitimacy is reinforced by religious responsibility: Han Guo protects [Quz Guo](quz-guo.md) and the sacred Taizhouist center at Shang.

## Current limitations

Several encyclopedia-level details remain open, including:

- full constitutional mechanics and factional structure
- precise demographic ratios by region
- administrative relationship between core republic institutions and remote outposts
