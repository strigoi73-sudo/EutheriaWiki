# Religions

Religion in Eutheria ranges from broad organized pantheons to region-bound traditions and state-linked churches. In many parts of the world, religious affiliation is inseparable from legitimacy, diplomacy, or civilizational identity.

This section works best when read alongside [Cosmology](../cosmology/index.md), since many Eutherian religions are built around shared questions of divine order, soul destiny, and the structure of the world. Articles such as [Pleroma](../glossary/pleroma.md), [Kenoma](../glossary/kenoma.md), and [Divine Power and Magic](../cosmology/divine-power-and-magic.md) help ground the metaphysical assumptions that many traditions take for granted.

[Skrosenism](skrosenism.md) and the [Rocciaganel Church](rocciaganel-church.md) are central for understanding religion in the post-Veltric world, especially where ecclesiastical authority overlaps with state and imperial legacy. [Ayedism](ayedism.md), [Delistanism](delistanism.md), and [Taizhouism](taizhouism.md) broaden that picture by showing how different regions construct ethics, ritual order, and sacred legitimacy. The [Eastern Nereth Religious Landscape](eastern-nereth-religious-landscape.md) and the [Brinese Order](brinese-order.md) then show how those traditions become lived regional systems rather than abstract doctrines.
