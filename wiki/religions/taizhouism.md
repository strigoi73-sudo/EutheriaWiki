# Taizhouism

Taizhouism is the major philosophical and religious tradition centered on [Shang](../states/quz-guo.md), the sacred heart of [Quz Guo](../states/quz-guo.md). It plays a major role in the self-understanding of [Han Guo](../states/han-guo.md) and the western Valtheran religious landscape.

Its sacred geography matters politically because Quz Guo's sovereignty is preserved less by raw power than by the religious importance of Shang itself. In practical terms, this makes Taizhouism one of the clearest examples in the setting of a religious tradition whose sacred center directly shapes state structure.

The wider Taizhouist world is dual in form: Shang functions as the sacred center, while Han Guo acts as the main political protector of that center. The relationship between republic and holy city is therefore part of the religion's lived public order rather than a mere diplomatic convenience.
