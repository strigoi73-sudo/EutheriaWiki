# Skrosenism

Skrosenism is the largest organized religion in the known world, with followers across three continents and especially deep roots among Flandric and related maritime peoples. Its durability comes in part from its decentralized character: the pantheon is shared, but local communities determine which deity stands at the center of worship.

## Founding tradition

The religion takes its name from Skrosen, remembered within the faith as prophet, priest, and king. Public scholarship ranges from literalist readings of his divine communion to more skeptical views that see a real historical temple-builder later mythologized into a foundational figure.

## Pantheon and practice

Skrosenism is a full polytheist pantheon religion whose deities map across the alignment structure. Good and lawful deities dominate mainstream practice, while Halmor anchors funerary tradition and the Grey Shore afterlife framework.

Institutionally, the religion has no single doctrinal head. Prestige flows through age, reputation, and temple importance rather than binding hierarchy. The oldest temples of [Garka](../states/garka.md) carry unusual weight in this prestige order.

## Cultural variants

The faith takes different tones in different societies. In [Likia](../states/likia.md), Skrosenism is often rationalist and maritime in emphasis. In the Flandric heartland it is more closely tied to founding memory, temple antiquity, and regional sacred tradition.
