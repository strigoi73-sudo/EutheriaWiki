# Cosmology

Eutherian cosmology describes the structure of reality, the planes, the soul cycle, the divine order, and the outer threat represented by the Kenoma and the Great Old Ones.

This section supplies the metaphysical foundation that underlies the setting's religions, sacred language, and much of its magical logic. Readers looking for the best point of entry should begin with [First Moment and the Structure of Reality](first-moment-and-the-structure-of-reality.md), then continue into [Planes of Eutheria](planes-of-eutheria.md) and [Souls and the Cycle of Existence](souls-and-the-cycle-of-existence.md).

[Divine Power and Magic](divine-power-and-magic.md) is especially useful for connecting abstract cosmology to lived practice, while [Kenoma and the Outer Gods](kenoma-and-the-outer-gods.md) explains the setting's public-facing account of outer threat and metaphysical danger. Readers may also want to keep the glossary entries for [Kenoma](../glossary/kenoma.md) and [Pleroma](../glossary/pleroma.md) close at hand, since those terms recur across both cosmological and religious writing.
