# Old Roman Beliefs

Old Roman Beliefs are the oldest surviving religious layer of the Roman-analog world in eastern Nereth. They predate the Republic and preserve a more ancient animist and folk-sacral understanding of land, household, ancestors, and local divine presence.

## Historical position

The tradition was never fully displaced, even during the height of the [Veltric Empire](../history/veltric-empire.md). It persisted in the Roman heartland as a living older layer beneath later religious and political orders.

## Present revival

Since the imperial collapse, Old Roman Beliefs have become more visible again. Their resurgence is not only theological. It also expresses distance from imperial institutions and skepticism toward the post-imperial claims of the [Rocciaganel Church](rocciaganel-church.md).

## Character

Old Roman Beliefs are best understood as local, ancestral, and place-bound rather than as a tightly centralized doctrinal system. Their strength lies in continuity of practice and rootedness in older communal memory.
