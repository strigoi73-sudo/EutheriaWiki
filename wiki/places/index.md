# Places

This section covers especially important cities, sacred sites, and campaign-scale settlements that matter beyond the normal level of state description.

Place articles are useful when a reader needs to understand where authority, trade, pilgrimage, or memory actually concentrate. They sit at a scale below the [States](../states/index.md) pages but often reveal more directly how power is experienced on the ground, especially in cities and sites whose significance exceeds their immediate hinterland.

[Marcici](marcici.md) is the clearest example, since it remains essential to understanding both imperial legacy and ecclesiastical authority. [Thornwick](thornwick.md) and [Tuwaid](tuwaid.md) show the same principle at a different scale: a place can matter because of trade position, sacred weight, regional access, or historical role even when it is not a continental capital. These articles are often most useful when read in tandem with [History](../history/index.md) and [Religions](../religions/index.md).
