# Likia and the Valtheran Naval Conflict

The long maritime rivalry between [Likia](../states/likia.md) and the old Kingdom of Xin Guo was one of the defining naval contests of the Likian Calendar's middle centuries.

## Rise and rivalry

Likia expanded from its Kasmoran coastal base into the Central Island Chain and built dominance over the main sea routes of Oceanus Centralis. As Valtheran naval states pushed outward, the Kingdom of Xin Guo became the principal challenger to that maritime order.

The struggle lasted roughly from the third to the eighth century LC as a long strategic conflict of naval campaigns, embargoes, privateering, island disputes, and shifting truces rather than one uninterrupted war.

## Settlement

Likia eventually prevailed. Xin Guo was dissolved and reconstituted as the [See of Xin Guo](../states/xin-guo.md), a reduced clerical state forbidden from maintaining a navy. The result was not reconciliation, but a two-century cold peace in which Valtheran states traded under Likian maritime terms.
