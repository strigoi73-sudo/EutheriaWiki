# See of Xin Guo

The See of Xin Guo is a small religiously governed state on Valthera's coast. It is the surviving rump of the former Kingdom of Xin Guo, once the principal Valtheran naval challenger to [Likia](likia.md) in the long struggle for control of Oceanus Centralis.

![Political map crop of the See of Xin Guo](../assets/maps/xin-guo-map.png)

## From kingdom to see

The Kingdom of Xin Guo was a major Chinese-analog naval and theocratic power during the long Likian-Xin Guo maritime rivalry. Before its defeat, Xin's ports also carried much of [Kaihui](kaihui.md)'s outward trade, making Xin's maritime system important even to neighboring states that were not naval powers in their own right. After Likian victory in the eighth century LC, the peace settlement dissolved the kingdom and reconstituted it as the See of Xin Guo.

This transformation was not voluntary. It was a deliberate settlement designed to eliminate Xin Guo as a naval threat by stripping it not only of its fleet, but of the legal right to maintain one.

## Present character

At 1026 LC, the See is small, religiously governed, and legally prohibited from naval activity. It survives as a reminder of defeat, but not as a reconciled or contented polity.

Its institutional ambition is now channeled less through military power than through aggressive religious and ideological projection. Current canon treats its proselytizing posture as a political outlet for frustrated state ambition under the cold-peace order.

## Place in the maritime order

The See of Xin Guo matters less for raw power than for memory and grievance. Its existence helps explain why later Valtheran naval powers chose accommodation with Likian maritime supremacy instead of direct challenge.
