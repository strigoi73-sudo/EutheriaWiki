# World of Eutheria

Eutheria is a pre-industrial fantasy world organized around four major continents separated and connected by **Oceanus Centralis**, the great central ocean. Geography matters at the highest level of world structure: mountain belts divide continental interiors, glacial zones limit settlement, productive plains sustain demographic centers, and maritime chokepoints determine which states can exert power beyond their own shores.

It is not a simple feudal patchwork. Eutheria is a world of preindustrial sophistication: commercially active, institutionally layered, and politically diverse, but still constrained by distance, difficult logistics, uneven state reach, and the hard limits imposed by terrain.

## Continental orientation

The current canonical continental orientation is:

- **Valthera** in the northwest
- **Nereth** in the northeast
- **Kasmora** in the southwest
- **Golsta** in the southeast

These are not equal in political or civilizational function. Some are centers of dense agrarian life and state competition; others are geographically severe and only marginal to humanoid political systems.

At broad scale, the continents also function as coherent cultural fields rather than as random assortments of isolated analog societies. Valthera is the main East-Asian analog field, Nereth the main Europe-like field, and Kasmora the main southwest-Asian and north-African-adjacent field.

## Core geographic structure

**Valthera** is a lake-rich northern continent shaped by interior waterways, cold northern extremes, and a durable balance among multiple strong regional states. Geography encourages regional power but resists durable continental unification.

**Nereth** is a continent of broken continentality. Major mountain systems divide its western coasts from its eastern heartlands, helping explain its fragmented political history, the uneven legacy of the Veltric imperial order, and the growing weight of western maritime trade.

**Kasmora** is the most agriculturally productive major inhabited continent and the demographic center of the known world. Its river systems, plains, and productive coasts support multiple major states, but no power has maintained stable rule across the whole landmass.

**Golsta** is the smallest major continent and differs fundamentally from the others. It is not a conventional humanoid civilizational sphere, but a largely glacial draconic wilderness with minimal settled political life.

## Oceanus Centralis and maritime order

Oceanus Centralis is not empty water between landmasses. It is the connective medium of the inhabited world, the space through which intercontinental trade, naval power, migration, and diplomatic contact become possible.

Its most important chokepoint lies in the strait between Valthera and Kasmora, where the [Central Island Chain](central-island-chain.md) allows control over the principal maritime routes linking the continents. Because of this, sea power in Eutheria is never only a matter of fleets. It is a matter of who controls islands, customs stations, anchorages, and the political terms of passage.

## World-historical pattern

Taken together, Eutheria's geography produces a multipolar world. Continental barriers prevent easy universal empire. Productive land is unevenly distributed. Maritime control can compensate for territorial modesty. Sacred centers, mountain passes, ports, customs throats, and grain basins all matter because they sit inside larger systems of dependence.

The result is a world where power is durable but rarely total, and where geography continues to shape history long after states and dynasties change. States are usually strongest at important nodes and weaker in provincial, rural, border, and wilderness seams.
