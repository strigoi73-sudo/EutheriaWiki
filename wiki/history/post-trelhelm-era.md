# Post-Trelhelm Era

The post-Trelhelm era tracks the long transition from civilizational recovery after **9034 BLC** to the fractured multipolar world of **1026 LC**. It is best understood as a sequence of uneven recoveries, state formation, imperial rise, and post-imperial rupture rather than as a simple upward march.

## Recovery period (9034 to ~2000 BLC)

Nereth and Valthera experienced prolonged demographic contraction, fragmented settlement, and low institutional density after the [Thousand-Year War](thousand-year-war.md). Kasmora recovered faster, aided by stronger continuity of agricultural practice through late glacial centuries.

## Rise of civilization (~2000 to ~624 BLC)

As climate stabilized and food surpluses expanded, durable kingdoms re-emerged across the inhabited continents. In eastern Nereth, Roman-analog polities gained durable administrative and military advantage. Kasmora's river kingdoms, nomadic confederacies, and coastal states consolidated into a long-term continental mosaic.

## Veltric Republic and Empire (~624 BLC to ~876 LC)

The Veltric Republic formalized regional dominance in eastern Nereth and later transformed into the [Veltric Empire](veltric-empire.md). Imperial institutions integrated large portions of eastern Nereth through roads, legal administration, and long-distance corridors, even though western Nereth remained outside direct conquest.

## The Age of Fracture (~876 LC to present)

The emperor's death in [Marcici](../places/marcici.md) without an heir initiated the [Age of Fracture](age-of-fracture.md). The period from 876 LC to the present is now best read through four phases:

- Immediate Collapse
- Wars of Settlement
- False Stabilization
- Deepening Fracture

This framework matters because the present world is not fully past the crisis. It is living in a later phase of the same long unraveling.

## Present context (1026 LC)

By 1026 LC:

- Nereth remains demographically dominant but politically divided
- western Nereth has risen through rerouted trade and mountain-pass mediation
- Valthera remains plural and strategically constrained by cold peace with Likia
- Kasmora's demographic and agrarian weight continues to grow
