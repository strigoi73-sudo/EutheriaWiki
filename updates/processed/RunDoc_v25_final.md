## 1. Canon Additions / Revisions

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Commonwealth of Sipharan — enclosure / border geography correction

**Primary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Part IX / Chapter 50 / The Commonwealth of Sipharan`  
**Secondary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Part IX / Chapter 50 / The Har-Sipharan Relationship`  
**Standalone Entry?:** no  
**Expected Action:** update  
**Current Coverage:** yes  

#### Summary Line
- Sipharan is a sheltered inner-gulf coastal commonwealth, but it is not enclosed on three sides by Har.

#### Geography
- The previous encyclopedia wording that Sipharan is surrounded on three sides by Har was incorrect.
- Sipharan borders Har to the north and northeast and Haria to the east.
- Sipharan remains open to the gulf to the south and southwest.
- It is coastal and sheltered on the inner gulf, but not geographically enclosed by Har.

#### Politics
- No political revision was established beyond the geographic correction.
- Future Sipharan prose should distinguish between sheltered inner-gulf position and literal enclosure.

#### Related named topics
- Kingdom of Har
- Kingdom of Haria
- Commonwealth of Sipharan

### [Scale: Region / State][Type: Canon][Status: New] Alaian-world religion / Kingdom of Alaia religion distinction

**Primary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Part IV / Chapter 35 / Altavism`  
**Secondary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Chapter 77 / The Kingdom of Alaia`  
**Standalone Entry?:** no  
**Expected Action:** update  
**Current Coverage:** yes  

#### Summary Line
- Altavism and the Brinese Order are distinct religious layers within the Alaian world and should not be collapsed into a single state-level assumption.

#### Core identity
- Altavism is the dominant organized polytheist faith of the Alaian cultural world.
- The Brinese Order is the dominant organized hearth religion of much of the Alaian world.
- These claims are not contradictory and do not imply that every Alaian-majority state is Altavist-dominant at the state level.
- Tazania remains the clearest state-level institutional expression of the Almaquegam Church.

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: New] Kingdom of Alaia — finalized cycle synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Chapter 77 / The Kingdom of Alaia`  
**Secondary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Alaian-world political comparison`  
**Standalone Entry?:** yes  
**Expected Action:** create  
**Current Coverage:** yes  

#### Summary Line
- Alaia is the principal mainstream monarchy of the Alaian world: a Brinese-majority Alaian crown kingdom influenced by the Veltric Empire without ever being fully absorbed by it.

#### Core identity
- Alaia shares much of its cultural and religious texture with the Fon, but is more formalized, more title-conscious, and more clearly organized around a real king.
- It preserved its native language and deep social identity because imperial influence reached it without fully incorporating it.

#### Politics
- Most Alaian nobles are small-landed rather than great magnates, while most Alaians are freeholders or live under local authorities whose subordination to a lord is often more formal than practical.
- The king is the recognized source of lawful rank.
- Local governance varies by district and by lord, with stronger direct lordly involvement nearer to Paga.
- Circuit courts carry petitions, grievances, and disputes beyond the safe competence of villages or local lords.

#### Economy / Society / Religion / Military
- Alaia’s material power rests on productive agrarian belts, abundant internal resources, and crown control of the Great Highway.
- Its principal internal resources are iron, timber products, and stone; the crown also controls a minor gold-extraction industry.
- The Great Highway is the lifeblood of the monarchy and is kept safe and moderately tolled to attract commerce, especially as one of the few southern routes bypassing the Dwarven toll passes.
- Alaia’s military is the diminished but durable descendant of an older anti-Imperial frontier system, now oriented toward preventing eastern instability from bleeding into Alaian territory.
- Paga is the last non-Imperial capital eastward before the old Imperial world; Etxetaharri is the principal eastern strongpoint, gate city, and military-academic center.
- Socially, Alaia is hierarchical without being deeply feudal; commoners are generally more respectful of monarchy than of local lordship, and the circuit courts help preserve this balance.
- Alaia’s warmest external relationship is with the Fon; relations with Tazania and Oteruenia remain underdefined; direct relations with Sinz are negligible because Dwarven-held lands intervene.

#### Related named topics

- Etxetaharri

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: New] Kingdom of Alaia — crown office structure and functions

**Primary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Chapter 77 / The Kingdom of Alaia`  
**Secondary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Alaian monarchy and institutions`  
**Standalone Entry?:** no  
**Expected Action:** create  
**Current Coverage:** yes  

#### Summary Line
- Alaia’s monarchy functions through a small but significant set of crown offices centered on household logistics, royal justice, documentary authority, highway order, and eastern military readiness.

#### Core offices
- Etxezaina (the Steward) oversees royal household provisioning, crown lands, and the material obligations that make monarchy function.
- Epaile Nagusia (the Chief Judge / High Justiciar) supervises the circuit courts and the higher judicial order of the realm.
- Mariskala (the Marshal) oversees the kingdom’s professional military coherence.
- Zigiluzaina (the Keeper of Seals) maintains confirmations, grants, seals, and documentary finality.
- Zaindaria (the Guardian) is responsible for the Great Highway as a kingdom-wide artery.
- Komandantea (the Commandant) commands Etxetaharri and its surrounding eastern defensive district.
- Akadamiaburua (the Master of the Academy) heads the military academy at Etxetaharri and preserves Alaia’s officer-training tradition.

#### Visibility and public life
- The offices most visible to ordinary people outside Paga are the Etxezaina, Epaile Nagusia, and Zaindaria.
- The Mariskala, Komandantea, and Akadamiaburua are most visible in times of tension or in the eastern belt.
- The Zigiluzaina is primarily an elite-facing office associated with confirmations, seals, title recognition, and legal finality.

## 2. Stable Interpretive Rules

### [Scale: Region / State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Revised] Alaian-world religion and state-level reading

**Primary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Part IV / Chapter 35 / Altavism`  
**Secondary Destination:** `Encyclopedia: Chapter 77 / The Kingdom of Alaia`  
**Standalone Entry?:** no  
**Expected Action:** update  
**Current Coverage:** yes  

#### Rule
- Do not collapse Altavism and the Brinese Order into a single state-level assumption for every Alaian-majority polity.
- Distinguish clearly between the Alaian cultural world and the Kingdom of Alaia when describing religion.

#### Practical implication
- Alaia should not be written simply as an Altavist kingdom.
- Tazania should remain the clearest institutional Almaquegam / Altavist state variant.
- Alaia should be treated as more state-shaped than the Fon but less church-structured than Tazania.
