# Valthera

Valthera is the northwestern continent of Eutheria, marked by cold northern extremes, lake-rich interiors, rugged uplands, and a durable multi-state balance of power.

## Physical character

- The far north is glacial and subarctic.
- The south and southeast include temperate forests, plains, and agricultural basins.
- Interior highlands and mountain systems divide the continent into connected but distinct regional zones.
- Lakes and rivers are core transport and economic arteries, not secondary routes.

## Peoples and contact zones

Valthera is structured by three major civilizational blocs:

- Chinese-analog polities across much of the south, east, and northeast.
- Korean-analog states concentrated in the west.
- Dwarven highland populations in interior mountain systems.

These blocs interact through frontier contact zones, especially in western and central uplands.

## Political pattern

Valthera is fragmented but not weak. Multiple strong regional states coexist, while geography resists durable continental unification.

A long-term external limit also shapes strategy: Likian maritime dominance constrains southern oceanic expansion by Valtheran powers, pushing competition toward internal waters, trade corridors, and regional influence.
