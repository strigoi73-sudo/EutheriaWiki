# States

The states of Eutheria vary enormously in scale, culture, and governing form, but they are all shaped by the same larger constraints: geography, religion, trade access, and the long aftereffects of imperial rise and collapse.

This section focuses on major contemporary polities rather than every historical regime. Some are continental powers built on land, grain, and population. Others are smaller but strategically decisive because they control ports, passes, sacred centers, or maritime chokepoints.

## Major patterns

- Large agrarian states derive strength from population, food surplus, and interior transport networks.
- Maritime states can wield influence far beyond their land area if they dominate shipping routes or customs corridors.
- Religious traditions matter politically. In several cases they shape legitimacy, diplomacy, and institutional culture as much as dynastic or military power does.
- No single state controls all of the inhabited world. Eutheria is a multipolar system structured by durable regional balances rather than universal empire.
