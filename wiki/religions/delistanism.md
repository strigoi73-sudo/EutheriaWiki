# Delistanism

Delistanism is a major organized polytheist tradition with especially strong political presence in western and southern Kasmora. The religion appears in sharply different institutional forms across states.

## Two major political expressions

### Dukanese theocratic model

In the [Dukanese Theocracy](../states/dukan.md), Delistanism is fused to centralized clerical sovereignty. Religious authority and state coercion are structurally integrated.

### Harian civic model

In [Haria](../states/haria.md), Delistanism is primarily embedded in communal and civic religious life rather than a full clerical state hierarchy. This produces a less coercive and more socially distributed pattern of authority.

## Why the contrast matters

The Dukan-Haria contrast shows that Delistanism alone does not determine state form. Institutional design, elite coalitions, and regional history strongly shape how the same religious tradition becomes political.

This divergence also drives diplomatic tension: Dukan can frame Harian practice as insufficiently orthodox, while Haria can frame Dukan's model as over-politicized religion.

## Minority and frontier patterns

Delistanist communities also appear in mixed-border contexts (for example, in Turkish- and Arabic-interface zones), demonstrating that religious identity in Kasmora does not map cleanly onto single-ethnicity state borders.
