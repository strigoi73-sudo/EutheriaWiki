# Dudbury

Dudbury is a broad inland English region within [Lienia](../states/lienia.md). It is loyal to the kingdom, recognizably civilized, and politically real, but not a region where the crown's direct attention naturally pools.

## Position within Lienia

Dudbury belongs to the kingdom's under-patrolled interior body rather than to its estuarial-commercial core. The kingdom is present there in law, tax, and symbols, but distant in daily practice.

Local people should think in Lienian terms and recognize royal authority, but they should not expect the crown to solve ordinary Dudbury problems quickly.

## Settlement and social order

Dudbury is large, sparse, and only lightly urbanized. Its capital is **Malton**, while **Olbore** functions as a secondary inland or upland town. Roads, villages, and market ties exist, but the region should not feel polished or heavily patrolled.

The count, local gentry, reeves, sheriffs, priests, and village headmen matter more to ordinary life than distant royal officers. If Dudbury has a problem, Dudbury generally handles it.

## Threat environment

Dudbury is a good example of moderate concealed creature pressure inside a civilized western kingdom. It is too long-claimed and too settled to support openly held humanoid realms, but too large and too lightly patrolled to have been fully cleared of hostile pockets.

Goblins are the baseline humanoid threat. Kobolds are also plausible in smaller concealed pockets, while hobgoblins and bugbears are better used as signs of unusual escalation.

## Regional summary

Dudbury is a broad inland region of Lienia: loyal, civilized, and recognizably part of a strong kingdom, but distant enough from royal priorities that its roads, villages, shrines, and uplands must largely survive on local order and local heroes.
