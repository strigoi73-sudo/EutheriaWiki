# Post-Trelhelm Era

The post-Trelhelm era tracks the long transition from civilizational recovery to the contemporary multipolar order (1026 LC). Canonical periodization emphasizes uneven regional development rather than a single linear rise.

## Recovery period (9034 to ~2000 BLC)

Nereth and Valthera experienced prolonged demographic contraction, fragmented settlement, and low institutional density. Kasmora recovered faster, aided by stronger continuity of agricultural practice through late glacial centuries.

## Rise of civilization (~2000 to ~624 BLC)

As climate stabilized and food surpluses expanded, durable kingdoms re-emerged across inhabited continents. In eastern Nereth, Roman-analog polities gained durable administrative and military advantage.

## Veltric Republic and Empire (~624 BLC to ~876 LC)

The Veltric Republic formalized regional dominance, then transformed into the Veltric Empire (~124 BLC). Imperial systems integrated large portions of eastern Nereth through legal-administrative infrastructure and long-distance corridors.

Despite scale, imperial penetration remained uneven: western Nereth stayed outside direct conquest, with mountain systems and Dwarven control of passes acting as enduring constraints.

## Collapse and successor order (~876 LC to present)

An imperial succession crisis around 876 LC accelerated fragmentation that had been building structurally for generations. No state fully replaced Veltric unity.

The resulting order is characterized by:

- contested successor legitimacy,
- partial continuity of imperial roads and legal culture,
- reconfigured east-west trade dependency,
- stronger relative importance of maritime chokepoints and mountain intermediaries.

## Present context (1026 LC)

By 1026 LC, the world is in systemic transition:

- Nereth remains demographically dominant but politically divided.
- Western Nereth rises through new trade roles.
- Valthera remains plural and strategically constrained by cold peace with Likia.
- Kasmora's demographic and agrarian weight grows steadily.
