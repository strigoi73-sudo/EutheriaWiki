## 1. Canon Additions / Revisions

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Lienia — Dudbury Corridor Importance

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/lienia.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** yes

#### State-level significance of Dudbury
- Dudbury should not be treated as one of Lienia's most exceptional regions in character.
- Its state-level importance is primarily **functional and geographic**, not spectacular.
- The main reason Dudbury matters above ordinary provincial background is that **Malton lies on the north-south road leading from Bolcescast south into Sinz**.
- This makes Dudbury more strategically relevant than its quiet agrarian profile would suggest, but the importance is concentrated along the corridor rather than spread evenly across the whole region.

#### Crown attention pattern
- The kingdom's interest in Dudbury should therefore be read as **unevenly distributed**.
- Crown attention is strongest where the corridor matters: safe transit, movement of messages and officials, provisioning, market continuity, and whatever wider diplomatic or military value the Sinz route carries.
- Off the trunk road, Dudbury still belongs more to local society, local institutions, and local problem-solving than to dense royal saturation.

#### Summary line
- **Dudbury matters to Lienia less because it is unusual in itself than because Malton sits on a road the kingdom cares about.**

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Dudbury — Typical Rural Region with Strategic Road Spine

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/regions/dudbury.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/lienia.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** yes

#### Regional character at state scale
- Dudbury should be treated as a **fairly typical rural inland Lienian region** in composition.
- It is agrarian, broad, lightly urbanized, and locally managed rather than socially or politically exotic.
- Its villages, manors, parishes, and minor road communities should feel like ordinary inland Lienia rather than a distinctive frontier civilization of their own.

#### The exception that defines its importance
- The region's one major distinguishing feature is the **Malton corridor**.
- Malton matters more than Dudbury as a whole, because it sits on the road linking Bolcescast southward into Sinz.
- This creates a useful contrast inside the region:
  - **on or near the corridor:** more traffic, more crown interest, more provisioning, more relay and message movement, and a stronger sense of the wider kingdom
  - **away from the corridor:** the more typical Dudbury pattern of local authority, thinner oversight, rural continuity, and under-patrolled seams

#### Summary line
- **Dudbury is a mostly typical inland Lienian region whose importance comes from placement: Malton sits on a road that matters to the kingdom.**

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Sinz — Stable State Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Summary Line
- **The Republic of Sinz is western Nereth’s harder civic power: a German-led territorial republic that turns strategic land position, layered provinces, and corridor control into leverage over movement between the western sea-gate and the Dwarven interior.**

#### Core identity
- Sinz should be understood first as **one of the two dominant western powers of Nereth**, paired with Lienia but playing the sharper and more openly assertive role within the western balance.
- The state is best understood not as a simple merchant republic, but as a **republic of corridors and provinces**: commerce matters because territory lets the republic govern, tax, redirect, and politically shape movement.
- Where Lienia projects steadiness, crown continuity, and maritime order, Sinz projects **civic hardness, mercantile appetite, legal pragmatism, and readiness to turn advantage into leverage**.
- Its republican form should not be read as democratic romanticism. At state scale, Sinz is better understood as a **patrician, oligarchic, magistrate-heavy republic** whose public language is civic and practical rather than royal or sacral.
- Sinz’s leading political culture is **German**, but the state should not be treated as culturally or confessionally flat across its whole territory.

#### Geography
- Sinz occupies a broad western-Nereth body between Lienia and several inland, eastern, and southeastern neighbors, with a western rivalry front, a large interior, and meaningful though secondary southern maritime access.
- The state is **larger than Lienia in land area**, but it has a smaller total population, fewer burgs, and lower urban density. It therefore reads less as a dense mercantile mesh and more as a **large territorial body governed through selected towns, roads, and provincial bargains**.
- Its **northeastern salient / panhandle** is strategically central. By projecting north and east between Lienia and the Dwarven mountain approach, it helps prevent Lienia from enjoying an uncontested direct land relationship with the western route system that leads toward the Dwarven passes.
- That salient gives Sinz leverage of several kinds at once: **customs leverage, transit leverage, diplomatic leverage, screening power, and bargaining power** within the western order.
- The north-central interior appears rougher, higher, and less uniformly settled than the lower southern and southeastern belts. This supports a republic organized through corridors and nodes rather than blanket administrative saturation.
- Fisch’s placement on the main west-east road inside Wutenken makes the republic’s center of gravity look **interior and mediating**, not frontier-parked and not purely coastal.

#### Politics
- Real power should be treated as resting with **councils, magistrates, provincial and territorial officials, major houses, and commercial blocs**, not with a single monarchical-style center of personality.
- Sinz is not a loose merchant league. It is a **territorial republic with real coercive capacity**, but one that governs unevenly and pragmatically.
- The republic’s political logic is to **territorialize commerce**: valuable corridors are secured, measured, taxed, and negotiated through institutions at the center and bargains in the provinces.
- The state’s mixed sub-jurisdictional forms — province, governorate, territory, tribe, and region — should be treated as evidence of **layered incorporation history**, not as decorative naming noise.
- Political authority is strongest in the capital province, on major roads, at revenue nodes, across the northeastern salient, and where border or customs pressure matters most. Away from those places, local society should retain more practical autonomy.

#### Economy / Society / Religion / Military
- Sinz is commercially aggressive in a harder, more instrumental way than Lienia. It should be understood as trying to **capture, redirect, tax, and pressure movement** rather than simply hosting a broad maritime gateway.
- Its economy should be read as a combination of **agrarian depth, inland movement control, selected coastal outlets, warehousing, customs extraction, and corridor service industries**.
- The republic’s larger geopolitical advantage is that it can help **mediate or interrupt the landward side of western trade**, especially where the route system running out of Lienia must eventually reckon with Sinz’s territorial position before reaching the Dwarven approach.
- Socially, Sinz should divide noticeably between:
  - the capital and a limited number of harder civic nodes tied into republican power
  - broad rural districts where local life is much less urban and much less politically central
  - peripheral or specially named jurisdictions whose relationship to the center remains visibly historical and uneven
- Religiously and culturally, Sinz should be treated as **mixed but governable**: a German-led civic order ruling a broader patchwork in which regional variation matters but does not erase the republic’s coherence.
- Militarily and diplomatically, Sinz should be read as a state that prefers **pressure over pageantry**: road leverage, customs leverage, fortified nodes, garrisoned interests, and readiness to convert economic advantage into political gain.
- Its rivalry with Lienia is a defining structural fact of western Nereth, but the relationship should not be written as constant open war. The two states are competitors inside a shared western system, with Lienia functioning more as the sea-gate and Sinz increasingly as the **hard-handed broker of the land-gate**.

### [Scale: Settlement][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Fisch — Inland Governing Capital of Sinz

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/settlements/fisch.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

#### Summary Line
- **Fisch is the inland capital of Sinz, placed in Wutenken on the republic’s main trunk-road system so the state can coordinate provinces, corridor revenues, and rival frontiers from a central mediating position.**

#### Site and function
- Fisch should be treated as a **central inland capital**, not as a western frontier city and not as a prestige seaport.
- Fisch lies in **Wutenken**.
- Its importance comes from placement on the main west-east road system, from workable connections into the southern and southeastern interior, and from its usefulness as a meeting point between provincial oversight and corridor governance.
- It is a plausible seat for the republic’s senate or ruling council, senior courts, state archives, tax offices, arsenals, relay administration, diplomatic residences, and the inns, workshops, and warehouses needed to support them.
- Fisch should also be read as the place where **route intelligence, customs intelligence, and provincial bargaining** most consistently converge.

#### Political meaning
- Fisch makes Sinz feel like a republic that rules a **country**, not merely a rival-facing corridor.
- Choosing Fisch as capital implies a state interested in **balancing regions, watching major trunk lines, and converting movement into policy** rather than defining itself by a single western frontier or by a coastal mercantile showpiece.
- Fisch does **not** need to be assumed the republic’s only great commercial metropolis. Its authority can rest primarily on capital function, centrality, and administrative concentration.

#### Urban character
- Fisch should feel more **bureaucratic, legal, and logistical** than ceremonially royal.
- Its public life is likely shaped by magistrates, clerks, merchants, military officers, provincial delegations, petitioners, hostels, archives, stables, and road-service industries.
- Even as capital, it should remain recognizably premodern rather than modern-bureaucratic in scale or built form.

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Wutenken — Province Containing the Capital

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/regions/wutenken.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

#### Summary Line
- **Wutenken is the province that contains Fisch; it matters because the republic’s governing center sits inside an otherwise ordinary provincial frame, allowing capital power and corridor governance to radiate outward through normal Sinz institutions.**

#### Provincial role
- Wutenken matters first because it contains **Fisch**, the state capital.
- Wutenken should **not** be imagined as a special modern-style capital district. It is better understood as a standard province whose importance has been transformed by housing the republic’s political center.
- The fact that Fisch lies in a **province** rather than a special capital territory is itself meaningful. It suggests Sinz governs from within an ordinary provincial frame rather than from a neatly segregated federal-style district.
- Wutenken’s provincial importance comes first from housing the capital and the institutions clustered around it, not from being Sinz’s largest province by area or population.
- In the corridor-and-province model of Sinz, Wutenken is where the republic’s **central institutions meet the ordinary machinery of provincial life**.

#### Local character
- Wutenken should feel more **road-linked, serviced, petitioned, and state-touched** than an ordinary Sinz province because the capital’s needs draw escorts, grain, records traffic, legal business, and provincial delegations into the area.
- Even so, it should not be imagined as heavily urbanized. Outside Fisch and a small number of other nodes, Wutenken still belongs to the same wider premodern fabric of villages, estates, parish life, crossings, and service settlements as the rest of inland Sinz.
- This makes it a good place to show how republican power radiates outward unevenly: strongest near the capital and trunk routes, weaker in the ordinary countryside.

#### Narrative use
- Wutenken is the cleanest first provincial exemplar for Sinz because it explains how the republic actually governs from its center.
- It provides a better state-defining lens than promoting an otherwise ordinary non-capital burg into false prominence.
- Any later Sinz exemplar should contrast with Wutenken rather than duplicate it: the northeastern salient, a southern/southeastern composite province, a governorate, or a tribal jurisdiction.

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Sinz — Regional Faces Framework

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Summary Line
- **Sinz is best understood not as a flat German mercantile block, but as a territorial republic composed of several interlocking regional faces whose value lies in how they feed, guard, or leverage movement.**

#### Major internal faces
- The first face is the **Wutenken–Fisch governing heart**: the inland trunk-road center where archives, courts, councils, taxation, and republican coordination are most concentrated.
- The second is the **western rivalry front** facing Lienia and the smaller western states. This is where Sinz’s competition with Lienia, cross-border commerce, and harder civic self-image are most legible.
- The third is the **northeastern salient**: the panhandle-like extension that helps separate Lienia from the Dwarven mountain approach and therefore gives Sinz disproportionate leverage over the landward side of the western trade system.
- The fourth is the **southern and southeastern composite belt**, lower and more outward-facing than the interior, important for agriculture, selected maritime access, and contact with southeastern neighbors. This face should not be written as simply German inland society continued to the shore.
- The fifth is the **interior seam belt** of rougher ground, mixed populations, and specially named jurisdictions. Here the republic’s layered incorporation history is easiest to see, and oversight is less likely to feel uniformly dense.

#### Practical value
- This framework lets the encyclopedia explain Sinz the way mature state chapters explain Lienia: not by listing random towns, but by showing how different parts of the state serve different functions.
- It also gives later province or settlement work a clearer standard: each sub-state topic should reveal one of these faces rather than compete to become an artificially central “main city.”

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Sinz — Do Not Flatten the Republic into a Dense City-State

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/_editorial/wiki-sync-notes.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- Sinz should **not** be written as a Venice- or Amsterdam-style city republic simply expanded over impossible acreage.
- Its republican form, commercial aggression, and strategic ambition do **not** imply dense urban saturation across the whole state.
- Settlement writing for Sinz should assume a realistic premodern hierarchy:
  - capital and a few major nodes
  - provincial or territorial towns and ports
  - market villages, crossings, parish centers, estates, and dependent rural fabric
- Broad rural space remains part of the republic’s character.

#### Practical implication
- State articles should emphasize **selected nodes and corridors**, not blanket urbanization.
- Regional articles should default to asking why a place matters: capital function, road position, customs, coastal access, provincial or territorial administration, or control of movement.
- The modest number of burgs relative to the state’s size should guide tone toward **territorial breadth and selective concentration**, not mercantile over-density.

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Sinz — Settlement Importance Must Be Earned, Not Assumed

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/_editorial/wiki-sync-notes.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- A Sinz settlement should not be promoted into a state-defining node merely because it is visible on a map, lies near a border, or briefly appeared in a stale CSV field.
- Elevation above ordinary-burg status should be based on **clear structural importance** such as:
  - capital function
  - major road junction
  - customs or warehousing role
  - provincial / territorial administrative role
  - coastal outlet significance
  - border leverage
  - historic memory that still has real consequences
- When those supports are absent, a burg should remain ordinary.

#### Practical implication
- This rule prevents false hierarchy inside Sinz and keeps the article aligned with the broader settlement-handling standard.
- It also means later Sinz development should prefer **state function and regional contrast** over map-induced town inflation.

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Sinz — Provincial Forms Reflect Layered Incorporation

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/_editorial/wiki-sync-notes.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- In Sinz, the coexistence of **province, governorate, territory, tribe, and region** should be treated as politically meaningful.
- These forms should be read as evidence that the republic absorbed and regularized different lands **without fully flattening them into one standard template**.
- The fact that the capital sits in **Wutenken**, an ordinary province rather than a special capital territory, is also meaningful: Sinz’s center need not be administratively exceptional to be politically central.

#### Practical implication
- Do not silently normalize every Sinz sub-jurisdiction into “province” in encyclopedia or wiki prose.
- Differences in form should invite questions about incorporation history, administrative inheritance, special oversight, or preserved local identity.
- This rule also supports portraying Sinz as a republic that is **practical and layered**, not cleanly uniform.

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Sinz — German-Led but Culturally Composite

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/_editorial/wiki-sync-notes.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- Sinz should be written as **German-led**, not as ethnically or confessionally uniform.
- The wider state includes a broader southern and southeastern body as well as smaller interior and peripheral differences that should not be erased in state-level prose.
- The German civic-political core therefore rules a broader territorial composite rather than a monocultural block.

#### Practical implication
- State-level prose should acknowledge both the **German republican core** and the **broader composite body** it governs.
- Later regional writing should show meaningful differences between the German interior/corridor face and the more outward-facing southern and southeastern belts.
- This keeps Sinz from becoming a mirror-image oversimplification of Lienia.

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Sinz — Corridor Leverage Is the Core of Its Power

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- Sinz should be written as a state whose deepest advantage comes from **leveraging movement through territory**, not merely from being commercially minded.
- The republic’s commerce matters because its land position lets it **mediate, tax, redirect, screen, and politically complicate** the overland side of western trade.
- In particular, the **northeastern salient** should be treated as a structural fact of state identity, because it helps separate Lienia from the Dwarven mountain approach and turns geography into bargaining power.

#### Practical implication
- State-level prose should compare Sinz and Lienia in **functional** rather than merely temperamental terms: Lienia as the steadier sea-gate monarchy, Sinz as the harder land-gate broker.
- Route systems, customs points, fortified nodes, and provincial bargains should be treated as central to how Sinz works, not as incidental detail.
- Later province or settlement articles should ask whether a place matters because it feeds this corridor logic, and if not, leave it ordinary.
