# Pre-Humanoid World

The pre-humanoid world of Eutheria reaches back to the age of dragons, the rise of reptilian civilizations, the wars between dragons and giants, and the earliest branching of the lineages that would eventually become the humanoid peoples of the present. This period explains the oldest civilizational layers of the world and the deepest foundations of later history.

## Earlier ages

In the oldest known eras, [dragons](../peoples/dragons.md) were the first intelligent beings and the dominant apex civilization. Reptilian temple-building cultures followed in their wake, treating dragons as divine or ancestral figures and leaving some of the oldest ritual sites in the world. The age of giants emerged as another major pre-humanoid phase of world history, marked by colossal monuments, roads, and fortress remains.

The proto-humanoid lineage diverged during these ancient periods, eventually producing both giant and smaller humanoid branches. From the smaller branch emerged the earliest elves, and later the wider humanoid family.

## Civilizational collapse

The Dragon-Giant Wars shattered both of the great apex civilizations of the ancient world. In the aftermath, elves emerged as the dominant surface civilization of the free peoples. Later history inherited a world filled with ruins from multiple forgotten ages rather than a single clean civilizational beginning.

## Historical legacy

The pre-humanoid world remains important because its ruins, myths, and metaphysical implications never fully disappeared. Later peoples built atop its remains, misunderstood much of what they inherited, and preserved only fragments of its chronology. That is one reason Eutherian archaeology is so layered and why the deepest past often survives only in scattered sacred sites, giant-made structures, and dragon-haunted memory.
