# Places

This section covers cities, sacred centers, and other named locations whose significance exceeds the normal scope of a state article. If the [States](../states/index.md) section explains how power is organized, the Places section shows where power, memory, pilgrimage, and local identity actually concentrate on the ground.

## Why Places Matter

Places in Eutheria are rarely neutral dots on a map. Capitals, shrines, ports, temple centers, market towns, and old administrative nodes often outlive the political arrangements that first made them important. A city may remain crucial because it still anchors a church, commands an estuary, preserves an imperial memory, or serves as the ritual center of a larger landscape.

This is one reason place articles deserve their own scale. They capture something more concrete than a continental or state overview, but often something more broadly significant than a local gazetteer. The question is not simply where a settlement is located, but why that place matters beyond itself.

## Major Examples

[Marcici](marcici.md) is the clearest example of a place whose importance far exceeds municipal description, because it connects imperial history, ecclesiastical authority, and the symbolic afterlife of the Veltric center. [Tuwaid](tuwaid.md) matters differently, as a sacred center tied to pilgrimage and religious orientation. [Thornwick](thornwick.md) operates at a smaller scale, but shows how a village can become a useful campaign reference point when it sits at the right intersection of local authority, parish life, and frontier pressure.

Place articles are often most illuminating when read alongside [History](../history/index.md) and [Religions](../religions/index.md), since many important locations derive their enduring significance from memory, ritual, or inherited institutional weight rather than from size alone.
