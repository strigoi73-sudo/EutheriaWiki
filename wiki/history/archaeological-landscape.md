# Archaeological Landscape

The archaeological landscape of Eutheria is defined by layered ruins from ancient draconic, giant, elven, wartime, and postwar civilizations. Many visible remains belong not to one lost age, but to repeated waves of occupation, destruction, retreat, and reuse.

This makes archaeology in Eutheria especially complex. Surviving ruins can combine multiple historical periods, later cultic reuse, collapsed infrastructure, and local myth in ways that obscure their original context.
