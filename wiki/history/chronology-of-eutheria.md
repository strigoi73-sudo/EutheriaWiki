# Chronology of Eutheria

This page summarizes high-level chronology used across the wiki. For analytical detail, see linked era pages.

## Deep and ancient eras

- **Prehistoric deep time**: planetary formation, tectonic restructuring, and continental separation.
- **Age of Dragons**: earliest known intelligent mortal lineage dominance.
- **Reptilian civilization strata**: major ritual-architectural complexes in warmer zones.
- **Age of Giants**: giant civilizational expansion and monument construction.
- **Dragon-Giant Wars**: mutual collapse of apex pre-humanoid powers.

## Catastrophe and recovery

- **~11,000 years before present**: Deep Folk crisis and long Thousand-Year War begin (approximate frame).
- **9034 BLC**: Battle of Trelhelm; TarKath defeated; Thousand-Year War ends.
- **9034 BLC onward**: Great Withdrawal (long glacial retreat) begins.
- **9034 to ~2000 BLC**: recovery period; severe northern contraction, relatively faster Kasmoran continuity.

## Classical and imperial phases

- **~2000 to ~624 BLC**: rising kingdom systems across inhabited continents.
- **~624 BLC**: Veltric Republic formalized in eastern Nereth.
- **~124 BLC**: transition to Veltric Empire.
- **0 LC**: founding of Likia; Likian Calendar epoch.

## Maritime conflict and collapse

- **~200 to ~300 LC**: Valtheran expansion into Oceanus Centralis accelerates, setting the conditions for sustained rivalry with Likia.
- **~300 to ~750 LC**: Likian-Xin Guo rivalry unfolds as a long strategic contest marked by intermittent naval wars, embargoes, proxy struggles, piracy, privateering, and competition over islands and trade routes.
- **~750 to ~800 LC**: final major Likian victory; Xin Guo reconstituted as the See of Xin Guo under naval prohibition; cold peace framework established.
- **~876 LC**: imperial succession crisis and beginning of terminal Veltric fragmentation.

## Present day

- **1026 LC**: current reference year. Successor-state order persists in Nereth; Likia retains chokepoint primacy; Kasmora's demographic-economic weight continues to rise.
