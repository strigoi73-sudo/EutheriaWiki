# Peoples

The peoples of Eutheria are not merely demographic categories. They are historical communities shaped by lineage, migration, language, religion, geography, and political memory. Some correspond closely to state majorities; others are dispersed across multiple polities, borderlands, or mountain zones.

This section is meant to document those communities as peoples in their own right, rather than treating them only as components of states or cultures.

## Topics this section will cover

- major ethnic and civilizational peoples
- highland, frontier, and diasporic communities
- peoples divided across political borders
- the relationship between ancestry, language, and civic identity
- how conquest, trade, and religion reshape communal identity over time

As the section expands, it will overlap with the state and culture pages, but its focus will remain on peoples as enduring human communities.
