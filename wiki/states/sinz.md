# Sinz

Sinz is the harder inland-commercial power of western Nereth: a patrician republic whose strength lies in corridor control, customs leverage, and the governable routes that connect western maritime trade to inland systems.

![Political map crop of Sinz](../assets/maps/sinz-map.png)

## Political character

Though formally a republic, Sinz is not a loose merchant league. It is a territorial, magistrate-heavy state with real coercive capacity, strongest at roads, customs points, provincial hearts, and high-value corridor zones. Its ruling tone is practical, watchful, and commercially aggressive rather than ceremonially royal.

## Historical place

Sinz emerged in 934 LC when the larger inland body of the old western kingdom broke with the crown and reorganized as a republic, while the ruling dynasty retained the coastal remnant that became [Fresen](fresen.md). That split still shapes western politics, because Sinz represents the inland-commercial successor order while Fresen preserves a dynastic remnant of the older kingdom.

## Internal structure

Sinz is not culturally uniform. Its Sinzian core is dominant in public identity, but the republic also includes a substantial Alaian south and a smaller Westric zone. These layers matter politically, especially where economic position, local loyalties, and religious difference intersect. The republic's strength lies in governing where power pays: routes, ports, customs points, and strategic corridors.

## Foreign posture

Sinz's rivalry with [Lienia](lienia.md) is one of the defining facts of western Nereth. It also remains structurally interested in [Fresen](fresen.md), whose survival preserves an older order beside the republic's own. The [Fon](fon.md) matters less as an invading threat than as a cultural and political reference point for the republic's Alaian south. Sinz therefore combines economic intelligence with a persistent sense of territorial grievance and western competition.
