# Geography

This section covers the physical world of Eutheria: its continents, major regions, maritime chokepoints, and the broad environmental conditions that shape settlement, trade, and political life.

The broadest point of entry is the [World of Eutheria](world-of-eutheria.md), which situates the continents around [Oceanus Centralis](../glossary/oceanus-centralis.md) and provides the large-scale frame for travel, exchange, and political separation. From there, [Valthera](valthera.md), [Nereth](nereth.md), and [Kasmora](kasmora.md) explain how continental geography produces very different civilizational and political patterns.

This section also helps readers understand why neighboring states can still feel like entirely different worlds. [Western Maritime Nereth](western-maritime-nereth.md), [Dudbury](dudbury.md), and [Rombevenia](rombevenia.md) show how coastlines, interior corridors, and regional boundaries shape identity and strategic pressure at a more local scale. The [Biomes of Eutheria](biomes-of-eutheria.md) article complements those regional pages by showing how climate and terrain influence settlement, movement, and frontier conditions across the setting.
