# Great Catastrophe and the Thousand-Year War

The Great Catastrophe and the Thousand-Year War mark the decisive end of the old ice-bound age and one of the deepest civilizational ruptures in Eutherian history.

## Deep Folk crisis

The war began when the Dark Folk alliance of drow, hryngar, and orc tribes launched the Deep Folk crisis from below. What followed was not a brief invasion but a thousand-year struggle that scarred the world and nearly broke the surviving peoples.

## Trelhelm and aftermath

The conflict ended in 9034 BLC at the Battle of Trelhelm, where General TarKath was defeated. At the same time, the Great Withdrawal began, initiating the long glacial retreat that opened the way for later recovery and new civilizational growth.

## Historical importance

This war sits at the hinge between ancient and later history. It explains not only the dispersal of peoples and the dark age that followed, but also the archaeological scars, trauma traditions, and mythic memory that continued into the present.
