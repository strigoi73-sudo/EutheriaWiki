### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Yan — Stable Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/yan.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/valthera.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



#### Identity and status
- Yan is a **legitimate but minor coastal duchy** on eastern Valthera's southeastern seaboard.
- It is not a sham polity, but neither is it a consequential regional power.
- Its ruling house emphasizes the dignity, continuity, and antiquity of the duchy chiefly for **domestic purposes**, not as an outward challenge to stronger neighbors.
- The correct tone is: **proud at home, cautious abroad**.

#### Historical origin
- Yan is the remnant of an **older kingdom now lost to time**.
- The older kingdom itself is gone, but the ducal house survived, and that survival is central to Yan's internal self-understanding.
- The duchy thinks of itself as preserving the remnant dignity of something older and larger than its present scale.

#### Survival through the Likian–Xin War
- Yan survived the Likian–Xin War largely because it was **too small and too inconsequential** to justify major intervention by the great powers.
- It remained cohesive, local, and self-contained enough that no major participant found it worth unmaking.

#### Geography and economy
- Yan is best understood as a **fishing-and-harbor duchy** with limited territorial depth.
- It has one respectable fortified port and a modest inland support zone rather than a broad strategic interior.
- Its economy depends mainly on:
  - fishing
  - fish processing
  - small coastal shipping
  - harbor service trades
  - modest inland agriculture
- It may benefit secondarily from littoral traffic, but it is **not** a toll-state, choke-point state, or major regional commercial engine.

#### Diplomacy
- Yan maintains **cordial but limited relations** with its neighbors.
- It seeks little entanglement and gives surrounding powers no strong reason to treat it as a problem.
- No major neighbor has a particular quarrel with Yan.

#### Religion
- Yan is conventionally **Xinist / Xinchang** in practice.
- It is not notably private and household-centered in the Kaihui manner, but it is also **not keen on the See of Xin Guo** or on outside clerical direction.
- Its religious life should be treated as inherited, local, and generally unexciting in political terms.

#### Military character
- Yan's military posture is limited but appropriate to a minor duchy:
  - ducal retainers
  - harbor guards
  - coastal watchmen
  - a few patrol craft
- It is better suited to **holding its own coast** than projecting force.

#### Summary Line
- **Yan is a small but proper coastal duchy: locally proud, regionally modest, and durable chiefly because it has preserved its dignity without forcing stronger neighbors to take notice.**

### [Scale: Continent][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Valthera — Continent-Level Synthesis Updates

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/valthera.md`
**Secondary Destination:** 
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



These points were strengthened or made explicit after v15 and should now guide any continent-level treatment of Valthera.

#### Whole-continent political character
- Valthera should not be described merely as a large northwestern continent with varied terrain.
- The stronger synthesis is that Valthera is a continent of **fragmented regional systems** rather than a unified political arena.
- It has **no dominant present-day hegemon**.
- The long **cold peace with Likia** still structures its maritime life: Valtheran states continue to trade through Likian-controlled waters under Likian terms.
- The destruction of **Xin Guo** remains one of the key events organizing the political logic of eastern and southeastern Valthera.

#### Regional division of the continent
Valthera now has a clearer three-part political reading:

1. **Western Valthera**
   - shaped by the Korean kingdoms and the Chinese–Korean sacred compact
   - anchored by Han Guo, Quz Guo / Shang, Cheonguk, and Hayanguk

2. **Central and Northern Chinese Valthera**
   - shaped by lakes, rivers, frontier distance, and unequal maritime access
   - anchored by Guan Guo, Longlin Guo, and Tengc

3. **Eastern and Southeastern Valthera**
   - the most intricate present-day cluster
   - anchored by Kaihui, Kan Guo, the See of Xin Guo, the Marches of Kai Guo, and Yan
   - defined by treaty corridors, plains, ports, clerical influence, and the remembered destruction of Xin Guo

#### Cultural synthesis adjustment
- Chinese-analog culture in Valthera should no longer be described too narrowly as simply "naval in character."
- The better synthesis is that Valtheran Chinese societies are **not politically uniform** and include:
  - river and lake states
  - coastal monarchies
  - republics
  - local littoral polities
  - confessional or clerical states
- Korean-analog culture should be understood as concentrated especially in western Valthera, where it supports both major kingdoms and inland religious states.
- Valthera as a whole should be treated as the setting's main East-Asian analog field, but with strong internal contrasts between settled Sinitic polities, Korean-analog western systems, and frontier horse cultures such as Kaihui.

#### Continental character statement
- The key continent-wide idea is:
  **Valthera is defined less by imperial succession than by fragmentation through geography.**
- Lakes, mountain corridors, broken coasts, and regional watersheds have allowed multiple medium and small states to persist instead of one enduring continental power.

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Eastern & Southeastern Valthera — Regional Synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/eastern-southeastern-valthera.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/valthera.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



This synthesis did not exist in v15 as a standalone codified regional frame and should now be treated as stable.

#### Core regional principle
- Eastern and southeastern Valthera are best understood as a **connected political system**, not a set of isolated coastal states.
- Its present shape was forged by:
  - the destruction of Xin Guo
  - the cold peace with Likia
  - uneven control of plains, ports, and coastal outlets
  - the strategic importance of narrow waters and sheltered transport corridors

#### Role of the major states
- **Tengc**: sparse northern fishery-confederation empire whose claims extend farther than its productive core
- **Kaihui**: imposed republic and great plains/horse/grain state, indispensable and vulnerable at once, with a self-consciously distinct horse-culture interior and a more Chinese-influenced coastal face
- **Kan Guo**: the region's clearest feudal hard power, fertile and politically legible
- **The Marches of Kai Guo**: treaty-administered littoral march controlling the sea outlet of a southern watershed shared by Kaihui and Kan
- **Yan**: minor but proper fishing-and-harbor duchy whose significance is mostly local
- **The See of Xin Guo**: small clerical remnant with outsized soft power through education, diplomacy, and intelligence

#### Religious-political gradient
- The See attempts to shape the wider Xinchang world, but its success varies sharply by state.
- **Kaihui** resists socialization of the faith outside its capital and especially across its low-literacy plains culture; this fits a society whose interior has long defended its own frontier horse identity against deeper absorption by neighboring settled civilizations.
- **Yan** is conventionally Xinist but not eager for See domination.
- **Kan Guo** appears religiously cohesive enough that See influence is more a matter of prestige than co-rule.

#### Practical geography of the region
- Kaihui's plains and valleys generate food, horses, and internal strength, but not full maritime autonomy; Chinese/Sinitic influence is strongest along its coast and shore-facing zones rather than across the whole culture.
- Kan's monarchy is more directly actionable and benefits from a more immediately usable coastal posture.
- Kai Guo matters because it controls the outlet of a southern watershed corridor, not because it is large.
- No polity in the region can be understood without reference to its waters.

#### Summary Line
- **Eastern and southeastern Valthera form a balance-of-systems littoral order defined by interdependence, grievance, clerical influence, guarded corridors, and remembered war.**

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Kaihui — Cultural Clarification

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/kaihui.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/valthera.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



Kaihui should be treated as a **frontier horse culture under long coastal-civilizational pressure**, not as a generic Chinese polity with steppe traits and not as a simple Mongol transplant.

#### Core identity
- Kaihui is primarily **Mongolian-analog** in cultural character.
- It also bears a significant **Chinese/Sinitic influence**, but that influence is strongest in its coastal and shore-facing zones.
- The interior should remain recognizably distinct from neighboring settled civilizations.

#### Social-historical pattern
- Kaihui is fundamentally a **grassland horse-people culture**.
- Its interior has spent long periods socially and culturally segregating itself from adjacent settled states.
- Its distinctiveness is not accidental drift; it is something the culture has actively preserved.
- Kaihui should therefore be modeled as highly defensive of its traditions, identity, and internal way of life.

#### Interpretive implication
- Kaihui should not be reduced to "Chinese with steppe traits".
- It should not be reduced to "Mongols transplanted into Valthera" either.
- The stronger synthesis is: **a deliberately self-preserving frontier horse society shaped by sustained contact-pressure from richer coastal civilizations without being culturally absorbed by them.**

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Likia — Cultural-Geographic Clarification

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/likia.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/kasmora.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



Likia should be treated as a **long-settled maritime hinge civilization**, not as a misplaced northern culture sitting accidentally in southern waters.

#### Core geographic identity
- Likia occupies the northeastern Kasmoran coast and the island chain in the strait between Valthera and Kasmora.
- This gives it a naturally central maritime role comparable to a thalassocratic hinge between major continents.
- Its strategic and commercial position should therefore feel more Mediterranean than merely northern.

#### Cultural formation
- Likia is a hybrid civilization originally shaped by:
  - Nordic-analog seafarers
  - and northern Kasmoran / Turkish-analog coastal peoples
- Likians have been established in Likia for at least a millennium, and likely longer.
- Because of that deep time, maritime centrality, and long internal development, present-day Likia can be culturally quite distinct from its roots without breaking plausibility.

#### Interpretive implication
- Likia's northern ancestral ties remain real and matter especially in relation to the Nordic Northwest and Skrosenist heritage.
- But Likia should not be imagined as a northern transplant that never adapted to its waters.
- It is better understood as a mature maritime civilization whose composite origins have been transformed by long residence, trade, and political centrality.

### [Scale: Continent][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Nereth — Continent-Level Synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Secondary Destination:** 
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



These points were developed after v16 and should now guide any continent-level treatment of Nereth.

#### Whole-continent political character
- Nereth should not be described as a single coherent political world.
- The stronger synthesis is that Nereth is a continent of **broken continentality**: a set of adjacent civilizational systems linked by trade, conflict, and geography, but not naturally unified.
- The continent's present-day balance is structured less by a single hegemon than by the interaction of:
  - the old eastern imperial heartland
  - the rising western maritime-commercial belt
  - the dwarven mountain intermediaries
  - the populous southern Castillian sphere
  - and the colder Nordic northwest

#### Master geographic fact
- The great mountain system dividing western from eastern Nereth is the continent's master geographic fact.
- The eastern lowlands and river valleys made empire possible.
- The western coastal and highland world developed outside that imperial core.
- The glaciated north remained harsher, thinner-settled, and more peripheral.
- The mountains are not merely a barrier; they are the hinge through which the continent must negotiate with itself.

#### Dwarven strategic role
- The dwarven mountain realms are not peripheral upland states.
- They are continent-shaping intermediaries because they control the principal trans-mountain corridors.
- Their importance increased after the weakening of the old southern maritime-overland trade route.
- In modern Nereth, no serious east-west commercial system can be understood without reference to dwarven pass control.

#### Historical core
- The defining historical fact of modern Nereth remains the rise and collapse of the **Veltric Empire**.
- Eastern Nereth should be understood not simply as fragmented, but as **incompletely de-imperialized**.
- Roads, legal forms, ecclesiastical authority, and legitimacy disputes all survive the empire's fall.
- The post-imperial east remains old, literate, structured, and unsettled rather than merely ruined.

#### Present-day redistribution of power
- The old Likian trade system once entered Nereth through southern Castillian ports and then moved eastward through imperial channels.
- That system was broken by the imperial collapse.
- In the present day, western Nereth benefits from a rerouted system in which Likian goods land first in the west, especially through Lienia, and then move inland toward the dwarven passes.
- This has shifted practical continental weight away from the old eastern core and toward the west and the mountain intermediaries.

#### External pressure from Likia
- Likia should be treated as an external but system-shaping actor in Nereth.
- It does not rule the continent, but it strongly influences its maritime trade, its western political balance, and the commercial rise of Lienia.
- Its religious and ancestral ties to the Nordic world of northwestern Nereth remain significant.
- At the same time, Likia should not be imagined as simply a northern transplant power. It is a long-settled maritime hinge civilization whose hybrid Nordic and northern-Kasmoran roots have been transformed by more than a millennium of rule over the strait world between Valthera and Kasmora.

#### Cultural plurality
- Nereth should not be summarized as merely the Veltric world plus peripheral western states.
- The continent includes major Roman, Italian, Castillian, Nordic, English, German, Portuguese, Anglo-Saxon, French, and Dwarven cultural zones.
- The Castillian south is too large and too populous to be treated as a footnote to the Veltric story.

#### One-line continent synthesis
- **Nereth is a continent-scale negotiation among broken imperial continuity, maritime trade realignment, mountain control, and enduring regional civilizational plurality.**

### [Scale: Continent][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Nereth — Stable Five-Region Framework

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Secondary Destination:** 
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



The following framework should now be treated as the default high-level regional division of Nereth.

#### 1. The Nordic Northwest
- The cold-margin, highland, and outer-coastal northwestern zone of Nereth.
- Distinguished by harsher climate, lower density, and deeper cultural continuity with the old northwestern seafaring world.
- Important not because it is the continent's demographic center, but because it preserves the ancestral highland-maritime zone from which Likia ultimately emerged.

#### 2. Western Maritime Nereth
- The rising western coastal-commercial belt of Nereth.
- Dominated by the English–German western sphere and energized by direct Likian maritime trade.
- Anchored especially by **Lienia** and **Sinz**.

#### 3. The Dwarven Mountain Hinge
- The central mountain world and its pass systems.
- Distinguished less by pure terrain than by the political leverage created by control of movement, tolls, and transcontinental trade.

#### 4. The Eastern Post-Veltric Core
- The old imperial heartland of eastern Nereth.
- Defined by fertile lowlands, imperial roads, ecclesiastical authority, and unresolved successor legitimacy.
- Organized by continuity-failure rather than by frontier emergence.

#### 5. The Southern Castillian Arc
- The broad southern and southwestern Castillian world of Nereth.
- Distinguished by large populations, significant climatic range, long relevance to maritime trade, and historical resistance to complete imperial absorption.

#### Framework principle
- This five-region structure is preferred because it cleanly distinguishes:
  - the **Nordic Northwest** from the more commercially central **Western Maritime** belt
  - the **Dwarven Mountain Hinge** from both adjacent lowland systems
  - the **Eastern Post-Veltric Core** from the **Southern Castillian Arc**
- It should be used as the default continent-level organizing scheme unless later state work forces a specific revision.

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] The Nordic Northwest — Regional Synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nordic-northwest.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



#### Core regional principle
- The Nordic Northwest is Nereth's cold-margin highland and outer-coastal zone.
- It is less important as a center of dense agrarian statecraft than as the surviving ancestral maritime and upland world of the northwestern peoples.

#### Geography and settlement
- The region is shaped by glaciated coasts, tundra and taiga margins, colder seas, and thinner settlement density than the western commercial belt or the eastern lowlands.
- It should be understood as a harsher and more peripheral environment, with state formation constrained by climate and terrain.

#### Cultural and religious character
- Nordic culture is concentrated here.
- The region preserves the ancestral highland-seafaring religious world associated with **Skrosenism** and the broader northwestern maritime tradition.
- This is one of the strongest cultural links between continental Nereth and the historical origins of Likia.

#### Political character
- The region reads as a belt of smaller and medium northern polities rather than a unified bloc.
- Its importance comes from maritime orientation, cultural continuity, and strategic edge-position rather than sheer population.

#### Summary Line
- **The Nordic Northwest is Nereth's ancestral highland-maritime margin: colder, leaner, and less central to empire, but culturally important as the preserved northwestern world of sea, upland rule, and old northern religion.**

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Western Maritime Nereth — General Regional Synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



#### Core regional principle
- Western Maritime Nereth is the rising broker coast of the continent.
- Its importance comes from acting as the point where **oceanic trade becomes continental trade**.

#### Geography and position
- This region is the western coastal and near-coastal belt that interfaces most directly with Likia and Oceanus Centralis.
- It is distinguished from the Nordic Northwest by a warmer, denser, more commercially integrated western littoral and hinterland.

#### Cultural pattern
- The dominant cultural sphere is the English–German west, with porous northern and southern edges.
- The northern edge grades toward the Nordic world.
- The southern edge grades toward the Castillian sphere.

#### Political pattern
- Western Maritime Nereth is politically more legible than the east.
- Its conflicts are primarily about:
  - ports
  - customs revenue
  - corridor control
  - commercial privilege
  - and relative leadership within a shared western sphere
- It is not dominated by the same existential successor-legitimacy conflict that shapes the Eastern Post-Veltric Core.

#### Major powers
- **Lienia** is the clearest maritime gateway power of the region.
- **Sinz** is the clearest inland-commercial and expansionist western power.
- Both should be treated as the principal anchors of the region.

#### Summary Line
- **Western Maritime Nereth is the continent's rising broker coast: a western English–German commercial belt whose power comes from ports, redistribution, corridor logic, and the rerouting of Likian trade.**

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] The Dwarven Mountain Hinge — Regional Synthesis

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/dwarven-mountain-hinge.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



#### Core regional principle
- The Dwarven Mountain Hinge is the controlled interior of Nereth.
- It is not merely a mountain barrier; it is the political conversion of geography into leverage.

#### Strategic role
- The dwarf realms control the principal east-west passes.
- They mediate trade, travel, and military access between the western coast and the eastern lowlands.
- This role became even more lucrative after the breakdown of the old southern maritime-overland trade route.

#### Historical consequence
- The mountain hinge is one of the main reasons the old republic and empire never simply absorbed the western world.
- The west could matter without conquering the east, and the east could not ignore the west, because the hinge prevented simple continental integration.

#### Political character
- The region should be treated as a pass-lord system in which tolls, route control, guarded corridors, and negotiated access matter more than broad lowland demographic weight.

#### Summary Line
- **The Dwarven Mountain Hinge is Nereth's controlled interior: a pass-lord mountain system that makes west and east interdependent while ensuring neither can easily dominate the whole continent.**

#### Core regional principle
- The Eastern Post-Veltric Core is the old heartland of organized Nerethan civilization.
- It is best understood as a **legitimacy basin**: the part of the continent where imperial law, roads, churches, and political memory outlived imperial unity.

#### Geography and settlement
- The region occupies the fertile eastern lowlands and near-coastal zones that once sustained the Veltric Republic and Empire.
- It is denser, more agrarian, and more institution-heavy than the western and northern systems.

#### Cultural pattern
- The region is dominated by the Roman and Italian cultural spheres, with a thinner Portuguese frontier belt.
- It is culturally coherent enough to function as a macro-region, but internally divided enough that no single ethnic reading can explain its politics.

#### Religious-political character
- The region contains the sharpest contest between the surviving **Rocciaganel** ecclesiastical order and the resurgence of older Roman traditions.
- Religion here is not merely devotional identity; it is legitimacy in political form.

#### Political pattern
- The region's states are rival claimants to inheritance, continuity, and civilizational seniority.
- **Polbium** embodies the strongest surviving sacred-imperial continuity claim.
- Other major states possess stronger ordinary sovereignty and territorial mass but weaker claims to speak for the old order.
- The result is a structured but unsettled successor system rather than pure chaos.

#### Summary Line
- **The Eastern Post-Veltric Core is the old imperial heartland turned contested inheritance system: fertile, literate, institution-heavy, and unable to agree on who now speaks for civilization.**

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Western Maritime Nereth — Sharpened Working Regional Reading

This synthesis should guide the first state-level pass within Nereth.

#### Core regional footprint
- The core western maritime-commercial belt is best read as:
  - **Lienia**
  - **Sinz**
  - **Bramia**
  - **Fresen**
- This region should not automatically include the colder and more northerly states better treated as part of the **Nordic Northwest**.

#### Regional logic
- Western Maritime Nereth should be understood as a **circulation-zone**.
- It grows by receiving, sorting, moving, taxing, protecting, and competing over flows of goods, people, and influence.
- The east is organized by inheritance and continuity; the west is organized by throughput.

#### Internal structure
- **Lienia** functions as the outer-facing maritime receiver and the principal landing point for Likian goods.
- **Sinz** functions as the inland-commercial consolidator and the western power most capable of turning trade flows into territorial influence.
- **Bramia** functions as a compact northern secondary coast and connector polity.
- **Fresen** functions as a southern frontier littoral state whose mixed edge-position helps connect the western commercial world to the southern Castillian sphere.

#### Cultural and religious gradient
- The region is culturally close-knit at the center but porous at the edges.
- English and German predominate in the core.
- Skrosenist religious alignment is especially strong along the northwestern and ocean-facing side.
- German and Castillian-adjacent religious layering becomes more visible in the inner-west and southern frontier.

#### Strategic tensions
- The region's main tensions are:
  - **Lienia vs. Sinz** over western leadership
  - maritime receivers vs. inland redistributors
  - dependence on Likia vs. fear of overdependence
  - and competition over the corridors leading toward the dwarven passes

#### Summary Line
- **Western Maritime Nereth is a rising broker coast led by Lienia's maritime gateway and Sinz's continentalizing republic, with smaller connector states buffering its northern and southern edges.**

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Restated] Lienia — Stable State Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/lienia.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



Lienia should now be treated as the **stable gateway monarchy of western Nereth**: an English-led maritime kingdom whose power comes from turning oceanic trade into inland influence while ruling a broad and unevenly integrated territorial body.

#### Core identity
- Lienia is one of the two dominant western powers of Nereth.
- It is the clearest **gateway monarchy** in the western trade system and the principal landing-point for Likian goods entering western Nereth.
- It is not an expansionist predator in the Sinzian mode. Its strength lies more in **population, urban density, maritime access, customs control, and durable institutional success** than in naked territorial aggression.
- Lienia should therefore be modeled as a kingdom of **functioning success** rather than frontier instability or post-imperial crisis.

#### Geography and maritime structure
- Lienia is a **broad maritime kingdom**, not merely a port-state attached to Bolcescast.
- Its power centers on **Bolcescast**, which sits at a narrow estuarial throat where movement can be watched, taxed, and organized.
- The kingdom's larger strength comes from controlling a **long articulated coastline** rather than one harbor alone.
- Lienia should be described as having four major geographic faces:
  1. **the Bolcescast estuary core**, where crown oversight, customs, maritime law, and international trade are densest
  2. **the secondary northern and western coast**, with older English coastal settlements and lesser ports
  3. **the southern and southwestern littoral**, rougher, more regionally textured, and partly tied to German settlement
  4. **the broad inland body**, which supports the coastal-commercial system but does not receive equal royal attention
- This means Lienia is maritime in identity but still deeply territorial in reality.

#### Cultural structure
- Lienia is not a flat English polity. It is a **composite kingdom**.
- Its internal cultural structure is best treated as three major blocs:
  - an **English crown-and-coast core**
  - a smaller **Anglo-Saxon inland and upland belt**
  - a substantial **German southern and southwestern bloc**, including towns and ports
- These are not just scattered minorities; they form meaningful territorial patterns.
- English culture provides the kingdom's core public identity.
- Anglo-Saxon culture should be treated as rural, old-fashioned, sturdy, and often judged backward by mainstream Lienian standards.
- The German population is different: economically useful, politically self-aware, and much more likely to imagine alternatives to English-led crown rule.

#### Religion
- Lienia's broad religious tone is **Skrosenist**, especially in the English and Anglo-Saxon zones.
- This helps tie Lienia into the wider northwestern maritime world and explains part of its ease of connection to Likia without making it a Nordic state.
- A meaningful **Germanist** belt survives in the German regional bloc.
- Religion in Lienia should therefore be treated as a source of regional texture and identity rather than of east-style legitimacy crisis.

#### Political order
- Lienia is a **crown-centered but unevenly reaching monarchy**.
- It should not be treated either as a simple feudal patchwork or as a modern bureaucracy.
- It is strongest where direct control pays clear returns: the capital estuary, major ports, high-value road corridors, and politically sensitive mixed regions.
- It is looser in much of the inland body, where daily order is mediated through local elites, burg authorities, priests, reeves, stewards, and customary practice.
- Lienia therefore exemplifies Eutheria's broader pattern of **preindustrial sophistication**: politically intelligent, institutionally layered, but not uniformly present.

#### Economy
- Lienia benefits directly from the post-Veltric trade realignment in which Likian goods land in the west and move inland toward the dwarven passes.
- Its strategic edge lies in:
  - customs and warehousing around Bolcescast
  - a chain of secondary coasts and ports
  - coastal shipping and fisheries
  - inland agricultural support and transport networks
  - and practical integration between ports, roads, market towns, and regional capitals
- The kingdom should not be reduced to a port strip. It is a **maritime-commercial crown built on a broad inland support body**.

#### Social character
- Lienia is cosmopolitan at the nodes and localist almost everywhere else.
- The crown-and-port world includes merchants, chartered town elites, customs officers, ship-service trades, and noble households close to court.
- Much of the inland kingdom is lived through priests, reeves, lesser gentry, village headmen, local market networks, and regionally rooted custom.
- This makes Lienia feel politically sophisticated without feeling modern.

#### Security and force
- Lienia should not be modeled as a conquest machine or a state that saturates every district with patrols.
- Its practical security profile is:
  - strong harbor and customs security where trade matters most
  - reliable protection on major roads and between key burgs
  - selective fortification of strategic nodes
  - enough force to keep the realm credible and make major disorder unattractive
  - but much thinner presence across large parts of the inland body
- Lienia's peace is therefore best understood as **corridor peace**: real and effective at high-value nodes, thinner in the seams.

#### External relations
- Lienia's most important external relationship is with **Likia**, whose goods it receives and whose maritime system it helps translate into western continental power.
- Its second defining relationship is with **Sinz**, the other great western power.
- Sinz is the harsher and more expansionist western competitor; Lienia is the steadier, more legitimate-seeming maritime monarchy.
- Lienia's external posture should therefore be modeled as commercially open, politically cautious, and highly attentive to the balance of western power.

#### The German question
- Lienia's German population forms a real **southern and southwestern territorial bloc**, not just a scattering of merchants.
- This bloc includes towns, ports, and a cross-border cultural horizon facing toward Sinz.
- Pro-Sinz or autonomist sentiment is therefore plausible, especially in **urban and mercantile circles**.
- Rural German populations are more likely to be practical and localist, caring more about burdens and protections than about constitutional programs.
- This means the kingdom's most serious internal cultural-political question is the **German question**, not the Anglo-Saxons.
- Lienia likely retains this regional bloc not out of sentimental tolerance, but because it is strategically and economically too important to surrender.

#### State self-image
- Lienia likely sees itself as:
  - **civilized**
  - **indispensable**
  - and **broader and steadier than its rivals**
- It probably prides itself on ruling a composite western realm and presents that diversity as proof of maturity.
- That self-image is not wholly false, but it also helps morally justify retaining provinces and towns whose attachment to the realm is not uncomplicated.

#### Summary Line
- **Lienia is the stable gateway monarchy of western Nereth: an English-led, commercially ascendant maritime kingdom built around the Bolcescast customs throat, a chain of secondary coasts, and a broad inland body, culturally composite, politically sophisticated, and strongest at the nodes that make it rich.**

### [Scale: Region][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Dudbury — Stable Regional Reading

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/regions/dudbury.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/lienia.md`
**Standalone Page?:** optional
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial



Dudbury is now the chosen **PC starting region** within Lienia unless later canon explicitly changes that choice.

#### Core identity
- **Dudbury** is a broad inland English region within the Kingdom of Lienia.
- It should be treated as part of the kingdom's under-patrolled interior body rather than as part of its capital or high-commercial core.
- It is loyal to Lienia, recognizably civilized, and politically real, but not a region where the crown's direct attention naturally pools.

#### Position within Lienia
- Dudbury is best understood as a region where **the kingdom is present in law, tax, and symbols but distant in daily practice**.
- It lies within the kingdom's territorial body rather than its most prestigious maritime face.
- Local people should think in Lienian terms, swear by the king's peace, and recognize royal authority, but they should not expect the crown to solve ordinary Dudbury problems quickly.

#### Settlement and communications
- Dudbury is very large, sparse, and only lightly urbanized.
- Its capital is **Malton**.
- **Olbore** should be treated as the region's secondary inland/upland town.
- The region has roads, villages, market links, and meaningful ties to neighboring districts, but it should not feel dense, polished, or heavily patrolled.
- It is exactly the sort of region where most of the land between towns still belongs to local society more than to active state supervision.

#### Political-social character
- Dudbury's working political assumption should be:
  - if Dudbury has a problem, Dudbury handles it
- The count, local gentry, reeves, sheriffs, priests, and village headmen matter far more to ordinary life than distant royal officers.
- Royal intervention should feel possible but exceptional.
- This makes the region ideal for hero work, because the state is not absent, but neither is it reliably coming.

#### Creature and humanoid pressure
- Dudbury should be treated as having **moderate concealed creature pressure** by Lienian standards.
- It is too civilized and too long-claimed by the kingdom to support large openly held humanoid domains.
- It is also too large, too sparse, and too lightly patrolled to have been fully cleaned of hostile pockets.
- Goblins in particular should be understood as a recurring regional problem rather than as a world-breaking threat.

#### Primary concealment terrain
- The most plausible survival zones for hostile humanoids and similar threats are:
  - upland folds and broken hill-country
  - woodland pockets and secondary forests
  - ravines, old tracks, and disused route systems
  - abandoned shrines, ruins, burial places, and old workings
  - and wide rural seams between villages, manors, and official patrol routes

#### Primary hostile types
- **Goblins** should be the baseline hostile humanoid problem in Dudbury.
- They should exist as bands, hidden warrens, road-feeding raiders, and opportunistic local predators rather than as a rival state.
- **Kobolds** are also plausible in smaller territorial pockets tied to earthworks, old workings, or stream-cut terrain.
- **Hobgoblins** and **bugbears** should be rare, and are best used as signs of unusual organization or escalating danger.
- Other threats may exist, but Dudbury's starting humanoid ecology should be built first around goblins.

#### Threat expression
- Creature pressure in Dudbury should most commonly appear as:
  - stock theft
  - attacks on isolated crofts and hamlets
  - roadside predation on minor roads
  - poaching and extortion in thinly watched woods
  - disappearances at shrines, ruins, and remote farms
  - and recurring local crises that are serious enough to matter to villagers, but too small to draw fast royal attention

#### Starting-area principle
- Dudbury should support the feeling that the PCs matter **first to the region, not to the kingdom**.
- Early authority figures should be regional and village actors rather than major royal or international ones.
- The region should feel close enough to civilization to be believable, but distant enough from the kingdom's priorities that hero work remains necessary.

#### Malton and the capital world
- **Malton** should be treated as a far-inland regional capital, not as part of Bolcescast's immediate capital basin.
- It belongs to Lienia's broad inland organizing body rather than to the dense estuarial-commercial core.
- Malton is tied to the crown by roads, law, tax, and regional hierarchy, but it should feel provincially removed from the daily assumptions of the capital world.
- This relationship matters tonally: Dudbury is not outside the kingdom, but it is far enough from the royal-commercial throat that local institutions usually absorb local crises first.

#### District settlement-density rule
- Large separations between **burgs** in Dudbury should not be read as evidence of empty land.
- The burg layer should be treated as the region's **higher-order urban and market network**, not as a full census of meaningful settlement.
- A Dudbury district cell in the Thornwick range can support many inhabited places without supporting many additional burgs.
- The realistic expectation is:
  - one or a few larger parish-service villages
  - several lesser villages, hamlets, crossing-clusters, mill-sites, granges, and manor-based settlements
  - and many isolated crofts, farmsteads, woodcutters' holdings, shepherd sites, and other small dependents
- In practical map terms, **the distance between burgs can stay large while the distance between ordinary inhabited places should be much smaller**.

#### Summary Line
- **Dudbury is a broad inland region of Lienia: loyal, civilized, and recognizably part of a strong kingdom, but distant enough from royal priorities that its roads, villages, shrines, and uplands must largely survive on local order and local heroes.**

### [Scale: Settlement][Type: Canon][Status: New] Thornwick — Stable Starting-Settlement Reading

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/settlements/thornwick.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/regions/dudbury.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** create
**Already in Wiki?:** no



Thornwick should now be treated as the default **starting settlement** within Dudbury unless later canon explicitly changes that choice.

#### Settlement class and role
- **Thornwick** is a substantial parish-green village of roughly **350 people**.
- It is **not** a burg and should not be treated as a true town, market borough, or urban node.
- It is best understood as a **head-village / parish-service settlement**: the dominant local village within its immediate district, large enough to gather worship, repair, storage, gossip, and customary authority without becoming urban.
- Thornwick should be the **only settlement in its class** in its immediate local area. Nearby settlements are dependents, not peers.

#### Physical character
- Thornwick should be imagined as a **village-on-a-green** with a broad communal center rather than a paved town square.
- Its key organizing features are:
  - the green
  - the parish church
  - a modest hall or manorial close
  - an inn
  - a smithy
  - a mill serving the local district
  - and a road-ribbon extension of cottages, sheds, and lesser households
- It should feel useful, muddy, lived-in, and socially legible rather than picturesque or fortified.

#### Economic and social function
- Thornwick exists to serve a wider ring of farms, crofts, shepherd holdings, wood-edge households, and dependent hamlets.
- People should come there for:
  - worship
  - milling
  - smithing and repairs
  - ale and lodging
  - local exchange and hiring
  - road news
  - and disputes too large for a hamlet to settle alone
- It is the nearest place where the surrounding countryside becomes locally organized.

#### District geography
- Thornwick sits in an **old, settled agricultural district** rather than at the edge of blank wilderness.
- The immediate country around it should read as:
  - an open cultivated core of fields, lanes, pasture, and parish land
  - broken by low ridges, brooks, smaller woods, and rougher margins
  - with larger concealment zones and stranger ground concentrated toward the district edges rather than in Thornwick's center
- The district should feel long-settled, named, and socially familiar, with older site-memory layered across ordinary working land.

#### Local settlement hierarchy
- Thornwick is the **district head-village**.
- Other nearby named places should be treated as dependent settlements or dependent sites rather than as rival village-centers.
- Current stable examples include:
  - **Hallowford** as a dependent crossing / chapel hamlet
  - **Alderfield** as a dependent field-and-manor hamlet
  - **Stonebrook** as a brookside or millside hamlet
  - **Olding Crossing** as a ford / bridge-side service cluster
- None of these should rival Thornwick in parish gravity, service capacity, or social centrality.

#### Threat structure
- Thornwick's danger profile should be modeled as **civilized-center / dangerous-margin** rather than as direct wilderness siege.
- The open core around the village is worked and recognizably ordered.
- Pressure comes from the edge-ring:
  - larger woods
  - broken ridges
  - abandoned workings
  - old burial or shrine landscapes
  - forgotten paths
  - and under-patrolled seams between settled places
- This is the correct scale for Dudbury's goblin problem: goblins survive in the district's concealment terrain and parasitize civilized movement without openly ruling the countryside.

#### Named district pressure-zones
- **Duskmire Wood** should be treated as the district's main concealment forest and the likeliest center of recurring goblin pressure.
- **Old Quarry** should be treated as an abandoned working and secondary danger-node: economically grounded, physically broken, and ideal for rumor, smoke, or hidden occupation.
- **Barrow Heath** should be treated as an older memorial / burial landscape whose danger is tied more to memory, taboo, and old ground than to ordinary farming use.

#### St. Cuthbert's Well
- **St. Cuthbert's Well** is not a true well but a **natural fissure** in the earth leading into a wider cave system that local tradition says ultimately opens into the **Underrealm**.
- The name persists from old local usage even though the feature is not literally a well.
- **Cuthbert** was not a real saint. She was a hermit woman, locally mocked with the title "saint" even before her death for her zeal and strangeness.
- She later fell into the fissure and died there. The nickname fossilized into place-memory, and the local joke hardened into a feared district legend.
- The site is now fully taboo to ordinary locals. No one goes near it willingly.
- Cuthbert's ruined homestead still stands nearby as little more than a stone foundation and a chimney.
- Her ghost or some spirit associated with her is held to haunt the ground around both the fissure and the ruin.
- Even goblins occupying the surrounding woods avoid the Well and the ruined homestead itself.
- Local adults still joke darkly about "throwing wayward children to St. Cuthbert," but this should be treated as black folk humor around a genuinely feared place, not as proof the site is socially casual.

#### Summary Line
- **Thornwick is an old parish-green head-village in a settled Dudbury district: the only true local center in a mesh of dependent hamlets and old named sites, secure enough at its core to feel civilized, but ringed by woods, ruins, fissures, and forgotten ground where danger still persists.**

Lienia should now be understood internally as a kingdom of several distinct but interlocking regional faces rather than as a flat English coastal monarchy.

#### Bolcescast Estuary Core
- The royal-commercial heart of the kingdom.
- Bolcescast controls the estuarial throat where movement can be watched, taxed, and organized.
- Crown authority is most direct here.
- Customs, diplomacy, warehousing, maritime law, and high mercantile activity are concentrated in this zone.

#### Northern / Western Secondary Coast
- An older English maritime belt of lesser ports and coastal towns, including places like Bermouth.
- Economically important but less intensely supervised than the Bolcescast basin.
- Crown presence is real but more indirect, operating through local elites, town influence, and selective corridor protection.

#### German Southern / Southwestern Littoral
- Lienia's most politically sensitive internal region.
- A substantial German territorial bloc with meaningful towns, ports, and maritime capacity.
- Urban separatist or pro-Sinz sentiment is strongest here, especially among commercial and literate elites.
- Rural populations are generally more pragmatic and localist.
- The crown retains this belt because it is strategically and commercially too valuable to surrender.

#### Southern Border-Facing Coast
- A working coast oriented toward Fresen and lower-prestige regional movement rather than royal display.
- Important for practical cross-border traffic, lesser maritime exchange, and local coastal economies.
- Crown oversight is lighter and more stability-oriented than in the Bolcescast core.

#### English Inland Body
- The agricultural and demographic backbone of the kingdom.
- Broadly loyal, slower-moving, and less politically intense than the great ports.
- Supports the maritime system through food, transport, labor, and inland market exchange.
- Much of the kingdom's ordinary life is lived here rather than in the harbors.

#### Anglo-Saxon Rural / Upland Belt
- A smaller but meaningful inland cultural zone.
- Treated by mainstream Lienian culture as old-fashioned, sturdy, and somewhat backward.
- More conservative, local, and only lightly engaged in wider state politics.
- Crown attention is limited so long as the region remains quiet and productive.

#### Interior Low-Control Seams
- Regions like Dudbury belong to this class.
- They are not outside the kingdom, but they are away from the nodes the crown values most.
- Patrol density is lower, local authority matters more, and problems often remain local unless they threaten revenue, roads, or prestige.
- These seams are the most plausible places for recurring humanoid pressure, local feuds, neglected shrines, road predation, and adventure-scale danger.

#### Structural Summary
- Lienia is strongest at **nodes**: the capital estuary, major ports, important crossings, and revenue-rich corridors.
- It is weaker in **seams**: rural interiors, uplands, lightly watched coast, and under-prioritized regions.
- This pattern should guide later development of both Lienia and similarly sophisticated western states.

## 2. Stable Interpretive Rules

### [Scale: World][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Revised] Map-Region Interpretation Rule

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/world-of-eutheria.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

For Azgaar-derived province layers, province nomenclature should **not** be treated as canon legal rank, feudal title, or formal administrative hierarchy unless later state-specific lore explicitly says otherwise.

Instead, these map units should be treated primarily as **regions**:
- coherent geographic areas
- settlement catchments
- cultural subzones
- practical travel and local-identity spaces
- or broad territorial envelopes useful for regional development

They may coincide with real legal or political jurisdictions in some cases, but that should be established separately rather than assumed from labels such as county, earldom, territory, captaincy, or land.

If a region has a listed capital in the CSV, that should be interpreted as the named burg exercising some meaningful organizing influence over the region — administrative, judicial, fiscal, military, religious, market, or logistical — even if the precise mechanism remains undefined.

#### Practical implication
- A named map-region such as **Dudbury** or **Rombevenia** should be treated first as a recognizable regional area within a larger state.
- That region may have some administrative, judicial, fiscal, military, or customary coherence, but it should not be assumed to be a literal county, duchy, or governorate unless later canon explicitly says so.
- This allows generated regional borders to remain useful for worldbuilding without forcing the setting into artificial title hierarchies.

#### Regional-capital rule
- If a region has a listed **capital** in the CSV, the named burg should be treated as that region's primary organizing settlement.
- This does **not** require a formal province government.
- It does mean the capital burg likely exerts meaningful influence of some kind over the surrounding region, potentially including market concentration, judicial or customary authority, tax or dues collection, patrol or mustering coordination, religious or shrine oversight, road, bridge, and message traffic organization, or simple recognition as the region's main local center.
- Regions without listed capitals should be treated as more weakly centralized, more thinly settled, or less clearly organized around a single burg.

### [Scale: World][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] World Character Principle — Preindustrial Sophistication

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/world-of-eutheria.md`
**Secondary Destination:** 
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

Eutheria should be treated as a **medieval magical world with politically and commercially sophisticated societies**.

#### Core interpretive rule
- Eutheria is not a simplistic feudal world.
- It contains layered monarchies, republics, transregional trade systems, mixed populations, inherited legal traditions, strong port cultures, and durable religious-political institutions.
- These are not exceptions or late anomalies; they are normal features of the setting.

#### What makes the world advanced
- Trade and commerce visibly shape geopolitics.
- States can be commercially integrated without being industrial.
- Political forms are diverse and serious rather than decorative.
- Ethnic and cultural blending is normal in port belts, trade corridors, frontier markets, and long-settled composite realms.
- Religion, law, and commerce can all operate at continental scale without requiring modern communications or modern bureaucracy.

#### What keeps the world preindustrial
- Communication is still slow.
- Logistics remain difficult.
- Geography strongly shapes political reach.
- Religion remains a major legitimizing force.
- State authority is uneven outside key urban, coastal, and strategic corridors.
- Rural provinces, borderlands, ruins, monster-haunted terrain, and hostile humanoid pockets still create real gaps in governance.

#### Setting design implication
- Eutheria should be understood as a world of **preindustrial sophistication**.
- Its societies have evolved far beyond standard fantasy-feudal stereotypes, but they still operate under the practical limits of terrain, distance, local power, magic, and uneven state reach.
- States in Eutheria should usually be strongest at important **nodes** and weaker in provincial, rural, border, and wilderness **seams**.

### [Scale: World][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Restated] Cultural-Geographic Placement Principle

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/world-of-eutheria.md`
**Secondary Destination:** 
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** review-only
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

Eutheria's cultural map should be treated as **regionally coherent by design** rather than as a random spread of isolated analog cultures.

#### Core interpretive rule
- Cultural analogs are not placed in isolation merely for novelty.
- Where possible, culturally related or historically comparable analog groups should sit near one another in ways that make broad geographic sense.
- This does not eliminate migration, hybridization, conquest, or enclaves, but it does mean the large-scale cultural geography of the world should read as patterned and intelligible.

#### Broad continental logic
- **Valthera** functions as the setting's main East-Asian analog field.
- **Nereth** functions as the setting's main Europe-like cultural field.
- **Kasmora** functions as the setting's main southwest-Asian / north-African-adjacent cultural field.
- These are broad organizing tendencies, not rigid monocultural boxes.

#### Setting design implication
- Neighboring analog cultures should usually have plausible relationships to one another.
- Mixed zones, contact belts, maritime hybrids, and frontier syntheses are encouraged where geography supports them.
- Cultural placement should therefore reinforce the feeling that Eutheria is a world with regional civilizational logic rather than a collage of disconnected references.

### [Scale: World][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: Revised] Region-Scale Creature Distribution Rule — Revised Nereth/Lienia Standard

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/world-of-eutheria.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/lienia.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

This principle should guide later regional development unless contradicted by later state-specific work.

#### Civilized-region rule
- In organized states, especially within the western kingdoms, hostile humanoids and comparable threats have largely been driven out of the best land and the most consistently patrolled corridors.
- Their continued presence should usually be explained by one or more of the following:
  - low patrol density
  - broken terrain
  - ruins or old sacred sites
  - marsh, forest, ravine, hill-country, or mine concealment
  - regional-border confusion
  - lordly neglect
  - or the profitability of feeding parasitically on nearby civilized traffic

#### Distribution model
- Creature pressure should generally be modeled in three bands:
  1. **strong suppression zones** around capitals, major ports, and heavily patrolled roads
  2. **recurring-threat zones** in secondary countryside, side roads, village belts, and administratively thin districts
  3. **concealed persistence pockets** in broken marginal terrain where hostile groups can actually lair

#### Lienian benchmarks
- **Rombevenia** should be treated as a benchmark for:
  - inner-estuary under-prioritized threat
  - low-to-moderate overall pressure
  - and stronger pockets in shoreline, marsh, and route-margin terrain
- **Dudbury** should be treated as a benchmark for:
  - inland regional under-patrol
  - moderate concealed humanoid pressure
  - and stronger persistence in uplands, woods, ruins, shrines, and thinly watched rural seams

#### Practical implication
- Sophisticated and commercially active states in Eutheria should not be treated as total-control societies.
- They remain strongest at important **nodes** and less complete in provincial **seams**.
- This principle should be used as a default for later western regional development unless a specific state is shown to have unusually high or unusually low local control.
