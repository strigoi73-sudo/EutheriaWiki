# Thornwick

Thornwick is a substantial parish-green village in eastern [Dudbury](../geography/dudbury.md). It is not a true town, but the dominant local service settlement of its district: large enough to gather worship, repairs, storage, lodging, and customary authority without becoming urban.

## Role in the district

Thornwick serves a surrounding ring of farms, crofts, hamlets, and dependent holdings. It is where the countryside becomes organized: where people come for worship, milling, smithing, news, lodging, and disputes too large for a single hamlet to settle.

## Tone

The village is old, useful, muddy, and lived-in. It belongs to a civilized but not heavily supervised inland region where local institutions carry most of the practical burden of order.
