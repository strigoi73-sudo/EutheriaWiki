## 1. Canon Additions / Revisions

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: Revised] Republic of Sinz — Stable State Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

#### Summary Line
- **Sinz is a German-led territorial corridor republic centered on Fisch, whose power rests on broad interior agrarian depth, legal-administrative control of roads and customs, full maritime access through its southern Castillian coast, and leverage over the foreland approach network leading toward the Dwarven mountain routes.**

#### Core identity
- Sinz is one of the two dominant powers of western Nereth, paired with Lienia but filling a different role within the same western order.
- It is an oligarchic territorial republic rather than a monarchy, with real power concentrated in councils, magistrates, patrician-commercial interests, and strategically important regional nodes.
- It should be understood as a broad inland-first route state, not as a simple merchant-city republic scaled upward.

#### Geography
- Fisch sits inland on the main west-east trunk-road system and should be understood as magistratic, archival, legal, and logistical rather than primarily maritime.
- The northeastern salient is best understood as a broad upland foreland approach network rather than a single neat pass; Sinz’s leverage comes from controlling the best-maintained and most governable routes through that system.
- The southern/southeastern Castillian belt contains the entirety of Sinz’s coastline and therefore the republic’s full maritime interface.
- The interior of Sinz provides much of the republic’s agrarian strength; the southern Castillian belt is important as a distinct lower and outward-facing face rather than as the sole breadbasket.

#### Politics / Economy / Society / Religion / Foreign posture
- Sinz governs unevenly, concentrating effective authority on major roads, customs points, warehouses, the capital core, and strategically sensitive districts rather than attempting blanket saturation of every locality.
- Its wealth rests on linked systems: broad agrarian support, inland exchange, customs leverage, warehousing, road services, selected ports, and upper-route control toward the east.
- The southern Castillian belt contributes materially to the republic but does not feel fully central to its German-led civic identity; this is one of Sinz’s defining internal tensions.
- Sinz is religiously mixed but publicly defined more by civic law, magistracy, and corridor administration than by confessional identity.
- To outsiders, Sinz is the western power most inclined to turn strategic position, access, and control of movement into leverage.

#### Regional faces
- **Wutenken-Fisch governing heart** — inland trunk-road core of courts, councils, archives, and principal coordinating institutions.
- **Western rivalry front** — belt facing Lienia and smaller western states, where customs scrutiny and western rivalry are most visible.
- **Northeastern salient** — politically decisive foreland approach network giving Sinz leverage over eastward movement toward the Dwarven routes.
- **Southern / southeastern Castillian belt** — lower, outward-facing, fully coastal face of the republic, important for maritime access, southern external contact, and persistent non-core texture.
- **Interior seam belt** — rougher mixed internal zones where local society remains more visible between the major nodes.

#### Related named topics
- Fisch
- Wutenken
- Lienia
- Dwarven mountain route system
- Western Maritime Nereth

### [Scale: Region / Historical Framework][Type: Canon][Status: New] Old western kingdom — Dissolution and successor settlement

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/history/nereth/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`; `wiki/states/fresen.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Summary Line
- **The old western kingdom that preceded Sinz and Fresen deteriorated under the combined strain of repeated rivalry with Lienia, the recurring German-question pressure in southern and southwestern Lienia, and mounting dissent in its own Castillian south; in 934 LC the larger inland body broke with the crown and reorganized as the Republic of Sinz, while the ruling house retained only its western coastal personal demesne, which endured as the Grand Duchy of Fresen.**

#### Core historical frame
- The old kingdom should be treated as the pre-934 LC polity from which later Sinz and Fresen emerged.
- Its crisis should be understood as part of the wider breakdown of lawful continuity and regional political order during the same broad generation as the Veltric Wars of Settlement, even though western Nereth itself was never under Veltric rule.
- The monarchy did not fail from one cause alone; it failed because external rivalry and internal composite strain reinforced one another.

#### Main pressures on the crown
- Repeated wars, border-commercial rivalry, and dynastic hostility with Lienia weakened the crown over time.
- The German populations in southern and southwestern Lienia formed a long-running bilateral grievance field for the old kingdom and later for Sinz: culturally kindred, politically sensitive, and repeatedly entangled with rivalry across the western littoral.
- Dissent in the kingdom’s own Castillian south further strained legitimacy, taxation, and political cohesion.

#### The 934 LC settlement
- In 934 LC, the larger inland territorial body broke with the monarchy and reorganized as the Republic of Sinz.
- The ruling house retained only its western coastal personal demesne.
- That surviving dynastic residue endured as the Grand Duchy of Fresen.
- Fresen should therefore be understood not as a random small survivor but as the recognized coastal dynastic remnant of the pre-republic order.

#### Interpretive implications
- Sinz is the larger territorial successor in transformed republican form.
- Fresen is the smaller dynastic survivor in continuous personal-rule form.
- Their relationship is therefore one of shared broken inheritance rather than mere adjacency.

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: New] Grand Duchy of Fresen — Stable State Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/fresen.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** create
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Summary Line
- **Fresen is a small German-led coastal grand duchy occupying the littoral hinge between Lienia and Sinz, sustained by moderate ports, a compact productive interior, and the inherited legitimacy of being the surviving coastal demesne of the old kingdom from which republican Sinz emerged.**

#### Core identity
- Fresen is not a third western great power alongside Lienia and Sinz; it is a secondary but durable western state.
- Its identity rests on dynastic continuity, coastal usefulness, and respectable survival rather than large-scale strategic dominance.
- It should be understood as the coastal dynastic remnant of the pre-934 western kingdom rather than as a random petty survivor.

#### Geography
- Fresen occupies the coastal hinge west of Sinz and south or southwest of Lienia’s main maritime body, with an indented shoreline and several moderate littoral openings rather than one overwhelming harbor complex.
- Its capital, Guhauben, should be understood as an inland-administrative ducal center rather than simply the largest port of the realm.
- The duchy’s scale is compact enough to be governable as a personal state, but large enough to feel proper and sovereign rather than petty.

#### Economy / Society / Culture / Religion / Foreign posture
- Fresen’s economy appears to rest on ordinary agrarian support from its compact interior together with fisheries, harbor labor, medium-scale coastal traffic, and local intermediation within the western littoral rather than on major customs hegemony.
- The state is German-led at the ducal core but includes a meaningful southern littoral Castillian layer that should be treated as a real coastal face without being inflated into a full dual-state crisis.
- Religion should be treated as locally embedded and ordinary rather than as a major ideological driver of ducal legitimacy.
- Fresen’s public tone is likely proper, cautious, and quietly proud: more local and dynastic than either Lienia’s maritime crown order or Sinz’s magistratic republic.
- To outsiders, Fresen appears respectable, useful, and durable — a secondary coastal duchy whose importance lies less in regional dominance than in old legitimacy, stable littoral function, and survivorship.

#### Historical position
- Fresen survives because it was the ruling house’s western coastal personal demesne at the time of the old kingdom’s collapse.
- Its grand-ducal standing should be understood as sovereign dynastic dignity preserved after the loss of the larger kingdom, not as evidence that it remained a kingdom in reduced form.
- Its relationship with Sinz is shaped by shared inheritance: Fresen preserves the dynastic residue of the old order, while Sinz embodies the larger territorial successor in republican form.

- Old western kingdom

### [Scale: World Principle][Type: Canon][Status: New] First Moment and natural history clarification

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/geography/world-of-eutheria.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/creatures/creature-origins.md`; `wiki/geography/cosmology.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** partial

#### Summary Line
- **The First Moment marks the metaphysical beginning of the metaverse, not a special-creation event in which Eutheria or its biosphere appeared fully formed; within Eutheria’s universe, natural law operates broadly comparably to our own, and life evolved over deep time, including dragons and other naturally evolved magical lineages.**

#### Core clarification
- The cosmological statement that the Material Plane and metaverse were formed at the First Moment should not be interpreted to mean that planets, ecosystems, or species within a given universe appeared in their final historical forms all at once.
- Eutheria’s universe follows ordinary natural law broadly analogous to that of our own universe. Geological history, biological evolution, and deep time all operate normally unless a more specific exception is stated.
- Eutheria therefore possesses a real natural history measured over millions of years rather than a recent whole-cloth creation of present-day life.

#### Creature-origins implications
- Dragons, ordinary animals, humanoids, and most magical beasts should be understood within the existing creature-origins framework as products of natural evolution inside the Material Plane, even where astral substance or magical conditions influenced that evolution.
- Some beings do arise by other mechanisms already established in the Encyclopedia — planar manifestation, supernatural transformation, or deliberate artificial creation — but these are exceptions within the creature-origins model, not proof that the world’s biosphere was created fully formed.
- References to the First Moment should be read as cosmological and metaphysical unless a passage explicitly discusses later biological, geological, or civilizational development.

#### Practical implication
- Future encyclopedia, wiki, and derivative summary language should avoid implying that Eutheria, dragons, or mortal species were created whole-cloth at the dawn of time unless a topic is explicitly one of the artificial-creation exceptions.
- Where helpful, cosmology-facing pages should distinguish between “the beginning of the metaverse” and “the later natural history of Eutheria within that metaverse.”

### [Scale: State][Type: Canon][Status: New] Grand Duchy of Bramia — Stable State Overview

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/bramia.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/geography/nereth/western-maritime-nereth.md`
**Standalone Page?:** yes
**Expected Action:** create
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Summary Line
- **Bramia is an ancient English grand duchy whose economy and historical importance are rooted in the river-mouth and lake-chain system opening onto its coast, making it a transfer state between Central Ocean shipping, the Norse-facing north, and the upper inland routes leading toward Sinz’s northeastern foreland.**

#### Core identity
- Bramia should not be treated as a Lienian split-state or dynastic leftover. It is an old state with an independent history and its own route logic.
- It is traditionally English in political identity and present-state cultural center, but long functioned as a crossroads between English and Norse worlds.
- Its distinctiveness lies in being a compact transfer duchy rather than a large sea-gate monarchy or a broad inland corridor republic.

#### Geography
- Bramia occupies a coastal-interior hinge north of Lienia, where sea access, an articulated littoral, inland waters, and overland approaches converge.
- The river-mouth and associated lake-chain opening into Bramian waters should be treated as the structural heart of the duchy’s economy.
- Holterden’s logic is administrative as well as maritime: it should be understood as the ducal control point of the hinge system rather than only the duchy’s largest port.
- Bramia’s terrain is serviceable and connected rather than mountain-hard, allowing it to function as a transfer and relay state rather than a barricaded marcher realm.

#### History and long-term role
- Before Lienia’s maritime rise, Bramia was an important transit and exchange state linking southern shipping, the Norse north, and inland movement toward the northeast.
- Lienia’s growth reduced Bramia’s relative prominence but did not erase its usefulness, because the Bramian river-mouth system remained a natural transfer interface.
- Bramia should therefore be understood as a state whose older importance declined relatively rather than collapsing absolutely.

#### Economy / Society / Culture / Religion / Foreign posture
- The core of Bramia’s economy is transshipment from ocean shipping into river-and-lake transport, together with relay trade, warehousing, harbor service, and ordinary agrarian support from its hinterland.
- Bramia is port-rich for its size, but it is not the dominant western sea-gate. It remains a significant secondary transfer state rather than a maritime hegemon.
- Though traditionally English, long Norse contact should be visible in port custom, seafaring culture, and coastal habits more than in a present-day demographic split.
- Bramia appears religiously cohesive and conventionally Skrosenist; religion should be treated as locally embedded and socially integrative rather than politically crisis-defining.
- Its foreign posture should be understood as cautious, orderly, and self-respecting: a state that survives by being useful, legible, and worth preserving rather than by dominating larger neighbors.

#### Regional faces
- **Holterden and the ducal harbor core** — governing and administrative hinge of the duchy.
- **The river-mouth / articulated littoral** — ports, transfer points, and the outward-facing economic heart of Bramia.
- **The service interior** — productive inland support belt linking coast, waterway system, and overland routes.

### [Scale: State][Type: Interpretation Rule][Status: New] Sinz — Region labels are geographic first, not constitutional proof

**Primary Destination:** `wiki/states/sinz.md`
**Secondary Destination:** `wiki/editorial/state-development-notes.md`
**Standalone Page?:** no
**Expected Action:** update
**Already in Wiki?:** no

#### Rule
- For Sinz, mapped labels such as province, governorate, territory, tribe, and region should be treated first as large geographic regional labels.
- They may preserve traces of layered incorporation, but they should not by themselves be used as decisive proof of exact constitutional status, formal autonomy, or specific internal administrative machinery.

#### Practical implication
- State-level synthesis for Sinz should privilege encyclopedia text, route geometry, coastline distribution, terrain, biome pattern, and settlement/population evidence over direct constitutional inference from label wording alone.
- The earlier tendency to read Sinz’s map labels as a self-explanatory constitutional chart should be treated as superseded.
- When future writing discusses Sinz’s composite nature, it should do so through geography, culture, corridor function, and uneven state reach rather than by overclaiming from region-label terminology.
