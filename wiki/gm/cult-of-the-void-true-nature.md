# Cult of the Void - True Nature

The Cult of the Void is not a normal religion, not a political faction seeking ordinary power, and not a heresy that fits within Eutheria's theological categories. It is a metaphysically broken infection-line founded by survivors of the late-Veltric translation of the tome.

## Origin

The modern Cult was founded in the final decades of the [Veltric Empire](../history/veltric-empire.md) by those who survived the translation of the tome and were broken open by its contents.

The Cult is therefore modern. The knowledge behind it is ancient.

## What it wants

The Cult does not ultimately seek thrones, borders, or lasting worldly rule. It manipulates institutions instrumentally because its true objective is the weakening of the structures that allow ordered existence to remain coherent.

Its real goal is the spread of Kenoma pressure through ordered reality: not merely the destruction of Eutheria, but the infection of the Material Plane, the Outer Planes, and ultimately the metaverse with the incompleteness that precedes them.

## Theological reality

Cultists draw clerical power from the [Great Old Ones](../cosmology/great-old-ones.md), which they understand as localized channels through which the Kenoma becomes present within the ordered cosmos.

They do not mistake the Great Old Ones for the final truth. The Great Old Ones are instruments. The void is the end.

## Method

The Cult does not recruit in ordinary terms. It infects. The insanity produced by the tome is not a side effect but the mechanism by which mortals become capable of carrying this orientation at all.

Because the Cult is oriented toward the breakdown of lawful continuity, its preferred targets are the largest lawful institutions of eastern Nereth:

- the Veltric Empire
- the Rocciaganel Church
- archives
- succession systems
- rites of recognition
- doctrinal trust

## Detectability problem

The Cult is exceptionally difficult to identify because its work registers as absence. Divination, planar scholarship, and outsider knowledge all fail to resolve it cleanly. The Kenoma does not appear as a detectable hostile presence within the ordered system. It appears as a hole in the system's ability to know.

## Current campaign use

The Cult is the central hidden antagonist of the setting's current campaign layer. Public history describes political fracture, religious contest, and unexplained disturbance. The hidden truth is that the Cult has spent generations turning the engines of Law into engines of fracture.
