# Divine Power and Magic

Divine power and magic in Eutheria follow stable cosmological rules. Divine magic is real, structured, and mediated through genuine sacred relationships rather than being merely cultural symbolism. Arcane magic is likewise an inherent feature of the world rather than a rare anomaly.

## Divine power

Religious traditions can differ sharply in doctrine and ritual, yet clerical magic operates across those traditions in ways consistent with the world's underlying metaphysical order. The existence of miracle-working clergy is therefore a real fact of the setting, not simply a matter of institutional prestige or symbolic belief. Different religions interpret divine mediation differently, but the underlying power is objective.

## Magic

Magic in Eutheria is part of the world's fabric. It is not so common as to erase the pre-industrial character of society, but it is common enough to be an enduring civilizational factor. Astral substance permeates living reality, which helps explain innate magical traits, hereditary bloodlines, and the long-term stability of magical items. Magic should therefore be understood as a natural extension of the structure of the universe rather than as an external exception imposed on it.

## Civilizational role

Because both divine and arcane power are real and structured, they influence education, warfare, healing, ritual life, and political prestige even in societies that are not dominated by overt mage rule. At the same time, high magical capability remains uncommon enough that ordinary social and economic life still retains its overwhelmingly pre-industrial character.
