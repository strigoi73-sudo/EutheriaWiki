# Brinese Order

The Brinese Order is the dominant organized hearth religion of much of the Alaian world. It emerged when older Alaian ancestor veneration fused with broader polytheist religious patterns without losing its household-centered emphasis.

## Character

The order is conservative, lineage-conscious, and domestic in tone. Its strongest concerns are burial, remembrance, obligations between generations, communal custom, and the proper relationship between household ancestors and the gods.

## Ancestors and gods

In Brinese teaching, honored ancestors are real and worthy of reverence, but always subordinate to the gods. This preserves older Alaian ancestral practice while integrating it into a wider sacred hierarchy.

## Political fit

The Brinese Order is especially well suited to societies that are culturally cohesive but only lightly centralized, such as the [Fon](../states/fon.md). It provides civilizational glue without demanding a hard central church.

## Alaian religious context

The Brinese Order should not be collapsed into [Altavism](altavism.md). In the Alaian world, the two operate as distinct religious layers: Altavism is the dominant organized polytheist layer, while the Brinese Order is the dominant organized hearth and ancestor-centered layer across much of Alaian society.

That distinction matters at the state level. A kingdom can belong broadly to the Alaian religious world without being defined simply as an Altavist state. This is one reason [Alaia](../states/alaia.md) should be read differently from more explicitly institutionalized Altavist polities such as Tazania.
