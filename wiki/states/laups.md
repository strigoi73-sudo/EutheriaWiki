# Laups

Laups is a broad Nordic upland kingdom of western Nereth. Its strength lies less in cities or maritime glamour than in holding together a large rough northern country through a few strong royal nodes, cohesive Skrosenist culture, and a monarchy built for sparse settlement and long endurance.

![Political map crop of Laups](../assets/maps/laups-map.png)

## Geography and structure

Laups occupies a large northwestern shoulder of western Nereth, with a long exposed coast, a rough upland and broken interior, harsher northern country, and a more workable southern belt.

It is best read as a broad upland kingdom rather than a pure mountain-wall state or a coast-first maritime power. Laups has maritime access, but its identity comes more from holding country than from concentrating trade.

## Capital and major nodes

The kingdom's political hinge lies at its southern approaches. **Aldved** serves as Laups's royal capital because it sits where inland waters, sea access, and the kingdom's most important external routes converge.

That does not make Aldved the kingdom's largest town. **Hvenesbak** should instead be understood as the larger commercial and demographic node, while Aldved retains royal and strategic primacy.

## Economy and frontier pattern

Laups rests on a broad, distributed material base: upland production, forestry, limited coastal traffic, hinge exchange in the south, and seasonal use of remote northern crown holdings. It is not a highly urbanized sea-gate kingdom.

Its border with Flaland is generally peaceful and socially porous. Minor grievances may be settled through bounded ceremonial battles rather than broader war, which makes the frontier feel shared and lived in rather than rigidly civilizational.

## Society and posture

Laups is culturally Nordic and religiously Skrosenist, with little sign of major internal ethnocultural fracture. Socially it should feel austere, proud, and more customary than bureaucratic.

To outsiders, Laups appears solid, somewhat hard, and more respected for endurance than brilliance: a northern weight rather than a glamorous power.
