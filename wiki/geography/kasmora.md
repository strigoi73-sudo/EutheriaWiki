# Kasmora

Kasmora is the southwestern continent and the most agriculturally productive major landmass in the known world. Vast plains, reliable rivers, and deep soils make it exceptionally well suited to large-scale food production and long-term demographic weight.

![Political map crop of Kasmora](../assets/maps/kasmora-continent-map.png)

## Geography

Much of Kasmora is relatively flat and fertile, with uplands concentrated more at the margins than in the interior. Rivers drain outward toward the coasts and support both irrigation and transport.

A northern mountain range separates the agricultural interior from the northern coast, which helps explain the coastal distinctiveness of states such as [Likia](../states/likia.md).

## Political landscape

Kasmora is politically stable by comparison with eastern Nereth. Its large states include the [Dukanese Theocracy](../states/dukan.md), the [Kordemelian Khaganate](../states/kordemeli.md), [Sala](../states/sala.md), [Haria](../states/haria.md), [Hawa](../states/hawa.md), [Har](../states/har.md), and [Sipharan](../states/sipharan.md).

The continent does not dominate world narratives in the same way as the Veltric collapse, but its demographic and economic trajectory makes it quietly decisive in the longer balance of power.

## Cultural and religious texture

Kasmora is home to Salan, Kordemelian, Rawran, Harian, Dukanese, and Likian peoples. Its religious landscape includes Ayedism, Delistanism, Skrosenist enclaves, and Rawran Society traditions, often crossing political boundaries in ways that do not map neatly onto state frontiers.
