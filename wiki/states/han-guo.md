# Han Guo

Han Guo is a Tengcian republic that is, in practice, a deeply integrated Tengcian-Cheoni union. Its institutional language and public form are Tengcian, but its political and social reality is more mixed and more cohesive than a simple ethnic label suggests.

![Political map crop of Han Guo](../assets/maps/han-guo-map.png)

## Political character

Han Guo is best understood as a negotiated republican compact rather than a conquest state. Its capital, **Insan**, is Cheoni, which strongly suggests that the Cheoni side of the union was foundational rather than peripheral.

The republic's official forms, court language, and civil tradition are Tengcian, which is why it is usually counted as Tengcian in broad reckoning. On the ground, however, the mixed settlement pattern and likely bilingual political life of the region indicate a more genuinely shared union than a simple dominance model.

## Sacred relationship

Han Guo's protection of [Quz Guo](quz-guo.md) is built into its religious and political self-understanding. Insan is the republic's political capital, while Shang in Quz Guo remains the sacred center of the wider Taizhouist world.

Han Guo's Tengcian character is expressed not through a Tengcian capital, but through its institutions, its Taizhouist religious identity, and its duty to preserve the autonomy of the sacred center.

## Geographic reach

Han Guo's northwestern holdings do not appear to form a second heartland. They are better understood as remote extraction outposts on the habitable fringe rather than as the basis of a broad northern territorial system.
