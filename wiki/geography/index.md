# Geography

Geography in Eutheria is not background scenery. Mountain barriers, lake systems, navigable rivers, biome distribution, and maritime chokepoints determine where populations concentrate, which states can project power, and how cultures remain connected or isolated over time.

This section begins with world-scale orientation and then moves inward to the major continents, strategic island systems, and broad biome patterns that structure life across the setting.

## Key themes

- **Oceanus Centralis** is the connective center of the known world. Intercontinental movement depends on sea routes, currents, and island passages.
- **Valthera** and **Nereth** are northern continents shaped by glaciation, mountain barriers, and strong regional differentiation.
- **Kasmora** is the most agriculturally productive inhabited continent and the demographic center of the known world.
- **Golsta** is an ecological extreme: a largely glacial draconic continent rather than a conventional humanoid civilizational sphere.
- The **Central Island Chain** is the single most important maritime chokepoint in the setting.
