# The Kenoma and the Outer Gods

The **Kenoma** is the metaphysical condition of incompleteness against which ordered existence appears as an anomaly. It is not simply destruction, corruption, or death within the ordinary cosmic system. It names the deeper possibility that the system itself can fail.

## Relation to ordered existence

The Kenoma stands outside the normal structure established in the [First Moment](first-moment.md). It is not another plane, nor another hostile force within the usual cosmic order.

For that reason, encounters with Kenoma-derived phenomena are not simply confrontations with stronger monsters or harsher planar environments. They are confrontations with pressures that ordinary metaphysical language handles poorly.

## Outer Gods

Outer Gods are native expressions of the Kenoma. They are not conventional beings, cannot be reasoned with in ordinary theological terms, and are not treated as normal statted entities.

This is not because they are merely very powerful monsters. It is because they do not belong fully to the same ontological category as creatures operating inside the ordered system.

## Great Old Ones

[Great Old Ones](great-old-ones.md) are localized crystallizations of Outer God pressure within ordered existence. They can be encountered more directly than the Outer Gods themselves, but mortal understanding of them remains fragmentary and often contradictory.

## Cosmological significance

The Kenoma matters because it defines a true exterior to the ordered cosmos: not another region within the structure of reality, but the possibility that the structure itself is contingent and vulnerable.
